## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1 Data Protection Laws**

"Data Protection Laws" means all applicable laws, regulations, and binding guidance relating to the protection of Personal Data, including:
- LGPD (Brazil)
- GDPR (EU/EEA)
- CCPA (California, USA)
- LGPD (Brazil's General Data Protection Law)
- Any applicable national, state, or provincial data protection laws

**1.2 Personal Data**

"Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("Data Subject"), including but not limited to:
- Name, email address, phone number
- IP address, device identifiers
- Health data (if applicable)
- Biometric data (if applicable)
- Any other information defined as "personal data" under Data Protection Laws

**1.3 Processing**

"Processing" means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, including:
- Collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage
- Adaptation or alteration
- Retrieval, consultation, use
- Disclosure by transmission, dissemination, or otherwise making available
- Alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction

**1.4 Data Subject**

"Data Subject" means the identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates (e.g., patients, users, employees).

**1.5 Sub-processor**

"Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

**1.6 Controller**

"Controller" means the natural or legal person which determines the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data.

**1.7 Processor**

"Processor" means the natural or legal person which Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

**1.8 Data Protection Authority**

"Data Protection Authority" means:
- **Brazil:** ANPD (Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados)
- **EU:** Supervisory authorities under GDPR (e.g., CNIL, ICO, Garante)
- **USA (California):** California Privacy Protection Agency

## 3. PROCESSOR'S OBLIGATIONS

**3.1 Lawful Processing**

Processor shall:
- Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller (unless required by law);
- Ensure that persons authorized to process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality;
- Not process Personal Data for any purpose other than as instructed by Controller.

**3.2 Security Measures (LGPD Art. 46, GDPR Art. 32)**

Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:

**Technical Measures:**
- **Encryption:** AES-256 encryption at rest, TLS 1.2+ in transit
- **Pseudonymization:** Where applicable
- **Access controls:** Role-based access control (RBAC), multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- **Audit logging:** Comprehensive logging of all Personal Data access
- **Vulnerability management:** Regular security assessments and penetration testing

**Organizational Measures:**
- **Privacy by Design:** Implement data protection principles from the design stage
- **Privacy by Default:** Only process Personal Data necessary for the specified purpose
- **Data minimization:** Collect only the minimum Personal Data required
- **Confidentiality agreements:** All employees sign confidentiality agreements
- **Training:** Regular privacy and security training for all personnel

**3.3 Confidentiality**

Processor shall ensure that all personnel who have access to Personal Data:
- Are subject to confidentiality obligations (contractual or statutory);
- Receive adequate training on Data Protection Laws;
- Have access only to the Personal Data necessary to perform their duties.

**3.4 Data Subject Rights Assistance (LGPD Art. 18, GDPR Art. 12-23)**

Processor shall assist Controller in responding to Data Subject requests, including:
- **Right of access** (LGPD Art. 18(I), GDPR Art. 15)
- **Right to rectification** (LGPD Art. 18(III), GDPR Art. 16)
- **Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")** (LGPD Art. 18(VI), GDPR Art. 17)
- **Right to restriction of processing** (GDPR Art. 18)
- **Right to data portability** (LGPD Art. 18(V), GDPR Art. 20)
- **Right to object** (LGPD Art. 18(§2), GDPR Art. 21)

Processor shall respond to Controller's requests for assistance within **10 business days**.

**3.5 Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)**

Processor shall provide Controller with all information necessary to conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) as required by GDPR Art. 35 and LGPD Art. 38.

**3.6 Audits and Inspections**

Processor shall:
- Allow Controller to conduct audits and inspections upon **30 days' written notice**;
- Provide Controller with all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA;
- Submit to audits by Controller or an auditor mandated by Controller;
- Make available to Data Protection Authorities all information necessary to demonstrate compliance.

## 4. SUB-PROCESSORS

**4.1 General Authorization**

Controller provides general authorization for Processor to engage Sub-processors, subject to the following conditions:
- Processor shall inform Controller of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of Sub-processors at least **30 days** in advance;
- Controller may object to the engagement of a new Sub-processor on reasonable data protection grounds within **14 days** of notification;
- If Controller objects, the parties shall work together in good faith to find a resolution. If no resolution is found, Controller may terminate the relevant service.

**4.2 Sub-processor List**

Processor shall maintain a current list of all Sub-processors and make it available to Controller upon request. The current list is provided in **Appendix E**.

**4.3 Sub-processor Obligations**

Processor shall:
- Impose on Sub-processors the same data protection obligations as set out in this DPA;
- Ensure Sub-processors provide sufficient guarantees to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures;
- Remain fully liable to Controller for the performance of the Sub-processor's obligations.

**4.4 Sub-processor Agreements**

Processor shall enter into a written agreement with each Sub-processor that includes data protection obligations equivalent to those in this DPA.

## 6. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION (LGPD Art. 48, GDPR Art. 33)

**6.1 Notification Obligation**

Processor shall notify Controller without undue delay (and in any event within **24 hours**) after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach.

**6.2 Breach Notification Contents**

Notification shall include:
- Nature of the Personal Data Breach (e.g., unauthorized access, data loss, ransomware);
- Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected;
- Categories and approximate number of Personal Data records concerned;
- Name and contact details of Processor's Data Protection Officer or other contact point;
- Description of likely consequences of the Personal Data Breach;
- Description of measures taken or proposed to address the Personal Data Breach and mitigate its possible adverse effects.

**6.3 Cooperation**

Processor shall:
- Cooperate with Controller in notifying Data Protection Authorities and Data Subjects;
- Provide Controller with all information necessary to comply with notification obligations under LGPD Art. 48 and GDPR Art. 33-34;
- Implement immediate remediation measures to contain and mitigate the breach.

**6.4 Breach Response Costs**

Processor shall bear all costs associated with:
- Breach investigation and forensic analysis;
- Notification to Data Subjects and Data Protection Authorities;
- Credit monitoring services (if applicable);
- Regulatory fines and legal fees resulting from Processor's breach.

## 7. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

**7.1 Retention Period**

Processor shall retain Personal Data only for as long as necessary to provide the Services or as required by law.

**Retention schedule:**
- **Patient health records:** 20 years (Brazil: CFM Resolution No. 1,821/2007; USA: varies by state)
- **Audit logs:** 6 years (HIPAA requirement)
- **Marketing data:** 2 years (unless Data Subject consents to longer)
- **Support tickets:** 3 years

**7.2 Return or Deletion (LGPD Art. 16, GDPR Art. 28(3)(g))**

Upon termination of Services or upon Controller's request, Processor shall:
- **Within 30 days:** Return all Personal Data to Controller in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format (e.g., JSON, CSV); OR
- **Upon Controller's written instruction:** Securely delete all Personal Data using industry-standard data sanitization methods (e.g., DoD 5220.22-M, NIST SP 800-88).

**7.3 Certification of Deletion**

Processor shall provide Controller with written certification that all Personal Data has been returned or deleted within **30 days** of termination.

**7.4 Exceptions**

If return or deletion is prevented by applicable law, Processor shall:
- Inform Controller of the legal requirement preventing return or deletion;
- Continue to protect the Personal Data and ensure it is no longer actively processed;
- Maintain the Personal Data only to the extent and for as long as required by law.

**8.1 LGPD Compliance (Brazil)**

Processor shall comply with all obligations under LGPD, including:
- **Lawful basis for processing** (LGPD Art. 7)
- **Data Subject rights** (LGPD Art. 18)
- **Security measures** (LGPD Art. 46-49)
- **Breach notification** (LGPD Art. 48)
- **ANPD cooperation** (LGPD Art. 55-J)

**8.2 GDPR Compliance (EU/EEA)**

Processor shall comply with all obligations under GDPR, including:
- **Lawful basis for processing** (GDPR Art. 6)
- **Special categories of data** (GDPR Art. 9) - if applicable
- **Data Subject rights** (GDPR Art. 12-23)
- **Security measures** (GDPR Art. 32)
- **Breach notification** (GDPR Art. 33-34)
- **Data Protection Impact Assessment** (GDPR Art. 35)

**8.3 CCPA Compliance (California, USA)**

If applicable, Processor shall comply with CCPA requirements:
- Process Personal Information only for business purposes specified in the Agreement;
- Not sell Personal Information;
- Certify understanding of and compliance with restrictions on sale;
- Cooperate with Controller in responding to consumer requests.

**8.4 HIPAA Compliance (USA Healthcare)**

If processing Protected Health Information (PHI), Processor shall also comply with HIPAA and execute a separate **Business Associate Agreement (BAA)**.

## 9. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

**9.1 Processor's Liability**

Processor shall be liable for damages caused by Processing that violates this DPA or Data Protection Laws.

**9.2 Indemnification**

Processor shall indemnify and hold harmless Controller from and against any and all claims, losses, liabilities, damages, fines, penalties, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of:
- Processor's breach of this DPA;
- Processor's violation of Data Protection Laws;
- Any Personal Data Breach caused by Processor's negligence or willful misconduct;
- Regulatory fines imposed by Data Protection Authorities due to Processor's actions or omissions.

**9.3 Data Protection Authority Fines**

Under LGPD and GDPR, Data Protection Authorities may impose significant fines:
- **LGPD:** Up to 2% of revenue in Brazil (capped at R$ 50 million per violation)
- **GDPR:** Up to €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover (whichever is higher)
- **CCPA:** Up to $7,500 per intentional violation

Processor shall be responsible for any fines imposed due to its Processing activities.

**9.4 Insurance**

Processor shall maintain cyber liability insurance with minimum coverage of **$5,000,000** per occurrence to cover data breach liabilities.

## 10. TERM AND TERMINATION

**10.1 Term**

This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the underlying Service Agreement or until all Personal Data is returned or deleted.

**10.2 Termination**

This DPA may be terminated:
- Upon termination of the underlying Service Agreement;
- By Controller with **30 days' written notice** if Processor breaches this DPA;
- Immediately by Controller if Processor suffers a Personal Data Breach affecting more than 500 Data Subjects.

**10.3 Effect of Termination**

Upon termination:
- Processor shall cease all Processing of Personal Data;
- Processor shall return or delete all Personal Data as instructed by Controller;
- Processor shall certify in writing that all obligations have been fulfilled.

## 11. MISCELLANEOUS

**11.1 Amendments**

This DPA may only be amended by written agreement signed by both parties. If Data Protection Laws change, the parties agree to negotiate in good faith to amend this DPA to ensure compliance.

**11.2 Governing Law**

This DPA shall be governed by:
- **For LGPD matters:** Brazilian law
- **For GDPR matters:** The law of the EU Member State where the Controller is established
- **For other matters:** The law specified in the underlying Service Agreement

**11.3 Jurisdiction**

Disputes arising under this DPA shall be resolved in the courts of:
- **Brazil:** Courts of São Paulo, Brazil (for LGPD matters)
- **EU:** Courts of the Controller's EU Member State (for GDPR matters)

**11.4 Order of Precedence**

In the event of conflict between this DPA and the underlying Service Agreement, this DPA shall prevail on matters relating to data protection.

**11.5 Severability**

If any provision of this DPA is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**11.6 Survival**

Sections 6 (Data Breach Notification), 7 (Data Retention and Deletion), 9 (Liability and Indemnification), and 11 (Miscellaneous) shall survive termination of this DPA.
