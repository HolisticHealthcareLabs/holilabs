## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1 General Terms**

Terms used but not otherwise defined in this Agreement shall have the same meaning as those terms in 45 C.F.R. Parts 160 and 164 (the "HIPAA Rules").

**1.2 Specific Definitions**

- **"Breach"** means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under the HIPAA Privacy Rule which compromises the security or privacy of the PHI, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 164.402.

- **"Business Associate"** means Holi Labs, Inc., as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.

- **"Covered Entity"** means the healthcare provider, health plan, or healthcare clearinghouse identified above, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.

- **"Designated Record Set"** means a group of records maintained by or for a Covered Entity that is used to make decisions about individuals, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 164.501.

- **"Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI"** means PHI that is transmitted by or maintained in electronic media, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.

- **"Individual"** means the person who is the subject of PHI.

- **"Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** means individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.

- **"Required by Law"** means a mandate contained in law that compels an entity to make a use or disclosure of PHI, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 164.103.

- **"Secretary"** means the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services or designee.

- **"Security Incident"** means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 164.304.

- **"Services"** means the AI-powered medical scribe platform, voice transcription, clinical documentation, SOAP note generation, billing export, and related healthcare technology services provided by Business Associate to Covered Entity, as described in Exhibit A.

- **"Subcontractor"** means a person or entity to whom Business Associate delegates a function, activity, or service involving the use or disclosure of PHI, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.

- **"Unsecured PHI"** means PHI that is not secured through the use of a technology or methodology specified by the Secretary in guidance, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 164.402.

## 3. PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES OF PHI

**3.1 General Use and Disclosure Provisions**

Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only as permitted or required by this Agreement or as Required by Law. Business Associate shall not use or disclose PHI in any manner that would constitute a violation of the HIPAA Rules if so used or disclosed by Covered Entity, except as otherwise permitted under this Agreement.

**3.2 Specific Permitted Uses and Disclosures**

Business Associate may use or disclose PHI to perform the Services for Covered Entity as specified in the Services Agreement, provided such use or disclosure would not violate the HIPAA Rules if done by Covered Entity.

Specific permitted uses include:

a) **AI Transcription and Documentation**: Use PHI to transcribe patient encounters, generate SOAP notes, and create clinical documentation using artificial intelligence and natural language processing.

b) **Clinical Decision Support**: Use PHI to provide clinical decision support alerts, drug interaction warnings, and evidence-based recommendations to assist Covered Entity's clinical workflow.

c) **Billing and Revenue Cycle**: Use PHI to generate billing codes (ICD-10, CPT), create charge capture documentation, and export billing information to Covered Entity's systems.

d) **Data Storage and Backup**: Store PHI on secure cloud infrastructure (AWS, Google Cloud) with encryption at rest and in transit, and maintain encrypted backups for disaster recovery.

e) **Analytics and Quality Improvement**: Use de-identified or aggregated PHI for internal analytics, platform improvement, and quality assurance, provided such data cannot be re-identified.

**3.3 Business Associate's Management, Administration, and Legal Responsibilities**

Business Associate may use PHI for its proper management and administration or to carry out its legal responsibilities, provided that:

a) The use is Required by Law; or

b) Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the person to whom the information is disclosed that:
   - The information will be held confidentially and used or further disclosed only as Required by Law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed to the person; and
   - The person will notify Business Associate of any instances of which it becomes aware in which the confidentiality of the information has been breached.

**3.4 Data Aggregation Services**

Business Associate may use PHI to provide data aggregation services to Covered Entity as permitted by 45 C.F.R. § 164.504(e)(2)(i)(B).

**3.5 De-identification**

Business Associate may de-identify PHI in accordance with the standards set forth in 45 C.F.R. § 164.514(a)-(c), and may use or disclose such de-identified information without restriction under this Agreement, provided that Business Associate does not attempt to re-identify the information or disclose the re-identification code.

## 4. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

**4.1 Compliance with HIPAA Rules**

Business Associate shall comply with all applicable requirements of the HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subparts A and E), the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subparts A and C), and the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart D) that apply to Business Associates.

**4.2 Prohibition on Unauthorized Use or Disclosure**

Business Associate shall not use or further disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by this Agreement or as Required by Law. Business Associate shall implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI.

**4.3 Safeguards**

Business Associate shall implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI that it creates, receives, maintains, or transmits on behalf of Covered Entity, as required by the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. § 164.308, 164.310, 164.312). Such safeguards are described in detail in Exhibit B ("Security Measures").

At minimum, Business Associate shall:

a) **Encryption**: Encrypt all ePHI at rest using AES-256 encryption and in transit using TLS 1.3 or higher.

b) **Access Controls**: Implement unique user identification, emergency access procedures, automatic logoff, and encryption/decryption mechanisms as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.312(a).

c) **Audit Controls**: Implement hardware, software, and procedural mechanisms that record and examine activity in information systems containing or using ePHI, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.312(b).

d) **Integrity Controls**: Implement policies and procedures to protect ePHI from improper alteration or destruction, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.312(c)(1).

e) **Transmission Security**: Implement technical security measures to guard against unauthorized access to ePHI transmitted over electronic communications networks, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.312(e).

f) **Workstation Security**: Implement physical safeguards for all workstations that access ePHI, including automatic screen locks, workstation use restrictions, and device encryption.

g) **Device and Media Controls**: Implement policies and procedures for the receipt, removal, disposal, and re-use of electronic media containing ePHI, including secure wiping or destruction.

**4.4 Mitigation**

Business Associate shall mitigate, to the extent practicable, any harmful effect known to Business Associate of a use or disclosure of PHI by Business Associate in violation of this Agreement or the HIPAA Rules.

**4.5 Reporting of Improper Use or Disclosure**

Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any use or disclosure of PHI not permitted or required by this Agreement, any Security Incident affecting ePHI, and any Breach of Unsecured PHI of which Business Associate becomes aware without unreasonable delay and in no case later than:

a) **Breaches**: Within 10 business days of discovery, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.410. The report shall include:
   - Identification of each Individual whose Unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed;
   - A brief description of what happened, including the date of the Breach and the date of discovery;
   - A description of the types of Unsecured PHI involved (e.g., full name, Social Security number, date of birth, medical record number);
   - Any steps Individuals should take to protect themselves;
   - A brief description of the investigation, mitigation, and corrective actions;
   - Contact information for Business Associate.

b) **Security Incidents**: Within 5 business days of discovery, including attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations. However, this provision does not require reporting of unsuccessful Security Incidents such as unsuccessful login attempts, pings, port scans, or denial of service attacks.

c) **Unauthorized Use or Disclosure**: Within 10 business days of discovery.

Reports shall be provided in writing via email to: [Covered Entity Privacy Officer Email]

**4.6 Business Associate's Agents and Subcontractors**

Business Associate shall ensure that any agents or Subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of Business Associate agree in writing to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to Business Associate with respect to such PHI, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.504(e)(2)(ii). Business Associate shall obtain satisfactory assurances that any such Subcontractors will appropriately safeguard PHI.

Current Subcontractors are listed in Exhibit C and include:
- Amazon Web Services (AWS) - Cloud hosting and database (HIPAA BAA in place)
- Google Cloud Platform (GCP) - AI/ML services and storage (HIPAA BAA in place)
- SendGrid/Twilio - Email and SMS notifications (HIPAA BAA in place)
- Sentry.io - Error tracking and monitoring (HIPAA BAA in place)

Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity in writing at least 30 days prior to engaging any new Subcontractor, and Covered Entity may object to such Subcontractor within 15 days of notification.

**4.7 Access to PHI**

Within 10 business days of a request by Covered Entity, Business Associate shall make available to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to an Individual, PHI in a Designated Record Set maintained by Business Associate for Covered Entity to enable Covered Entity to fulfill its obligations under 45 C.F.R. § 164.524 (right of access).

If Business Associate maintains ePHI in one or more Designated Record Sets electronically, Business Associate shall provide such information in electronic format to enable Covered Entity to fulfill its obligations under 45 C.F.R. § 164.524(c)(2)(ii).

**4.8 Amendment of PHI**

Within 10 business days of receipt of a request from Covered Entity, Business Associate shall make available PHI in a Designated Record Set maintained by Business Associate for amendment and incorporate any amendments to PHI as directed by Covered Entity pursuant to 45 C.F.R. § 164.526.

**4.9 Accounting of Disclosures**

Within 20 business days of a request by Covered Entity, Business Associate shall make available to Covered Entity the information required to provide an accounting of disclosures of PHI, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.528, including:

a) The date of the disclosure;
b) The name and address (if known) of the recipient;
c) A brief description of the PHI disclosed;
d) A brief statement of the purpose of the disclosure.

Business Associate shall maintain records of all disclosures of PHI for at least six (6) years from the date of disclosure.

**4.10 Availability of Books and Records**

Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary for purposes of determining compliance with the HIPAA Rules, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.504(e)(2)(ii)(I). Business Associate shall provide such access within 10 business days of a request by the Secretary.

**4.11 Minimum Necessary**

Business Associate shall make reasonable efforts to use, disclose, and request only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose of the use, disclosure, or request, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.502(b) and 45 C.F.R. § 164.514(d).

## 7. BREACH NOTIFICATION

**7.1 Discovery and Notification**

Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity without unreasonable delay, and in no case later than 10 business days, after discovery of a Breach of Unsecured PHI, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.410.

"Discovery" is deemed to have occurred as of the first day on which the Breach is known, or reasonably should have been known, to Business Associate or any employee, officer, or agent of Business Associate (other than the person committing the Breach).

**7.2 Content of Notification**

The Breach notification shall include, to the extent possible:

a) Identification of each Individual whose Unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed during the Breach;

b) A brief description of what happened, including the date of the Breach (if known) and the date of discovery of the Breach;

c) A description of the types of Unsecured PHI that were involved in the Breach (such as full name, Social Security number, date of birth, home address, account number, diagnosis, disability code, or other types of information);

d) Any steps Business Associate recommends that Individuals take to protect themselves from potential harm resulting from the Breach;

e) A brief description of what Business Associate is doing to investigate the Breach, to mitigate harm to Individuals, and to protect against any further Breaches;

f) Contact procedures for Individuals to ask questions or learn additional information, including a toll-free telephone number, email address, website, or postal address.

**7.3 Investigation and Mitigation**

Business Associate shall promptly investigate any suspected or actual Breach and shall take appropriate steps to mitigate, to the extent practicable, any harmful effects of the Breach. Business Associate shall provide Covered Entity with a written report of its investigation and remediation efforts within 30 days of discovery.

**7.4 Cooperation with Individual Notification**

Business Associate shall cooperate with Covered Entity in fulfilling Covered Entity's obligations to notify affected Individuals, the Secretary, and (if applicable) the media, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.404-408. This may include providing mailing lists, contact information, or other assistance requested by Covered Entity.

**7.5 Costs**

Business Associate shall be responsible for all costs associated with any Breach caused by Business Associate's acts or omissions, including but not limited to:
- Investigation and forensic analysis
- Legal fees and regulatory response
- Individual notification costs (mailing, call center)
- Credit monitoring services for affected Individuals (if applicable)
- Media notification (if applicable)
- Regulatory fines and penalties attributable to Business Associate's actions
- Settlement costs and damages

**7.6 Breach Harm Analysis**

For purposes of determining whether a Breach has occurred, Business Associate shall perform a risk assessment in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.402(2) and provide the results to Covered Entity. The assessment shall consider at least the following factors:

a) The nature and extent of the PHI involved, including the types of identifiers and the likelihood of re-identification;
b) The unauthorized person who used the PHI or to whom the disclosure was made;
c) Whether the PHI was actually acquired or viewed;
d) The extent to which the risk to the PHI has been mitigated.

## 8. SECURITY INCIDENT MANAGEMENT

**8.1 Security Incident Response**

Business Associate shall implement and maintain a security incident response plan that includes procedures for detecting, analyzing, containing, eradicating, and recovering from Security Incidents, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.308(a)(6).

**8.2 Reporting Security Incidents**

Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any successful Security Incident that results in unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of ePHI within 5 business days of discovery.

This obligation does not require Business Associate to report routine Security Incidents such as:
- Unsuccessful login attempts
- Port scans
- Pings or other broadcast attacks on firewall
- Denial of service attacks that do not result in data access

**8.3 Root Cause Analysis**

For any reportable Security Incident, Business Associate shall conduct a root cause analysis and provide Covered Entity with a written report within 30 days that includes:
- Description of the incident
- Timeline of events
- Systems and data affected
- Root cause determination
- Corrective actions taken
- Preventive measures implemented

## 11. TERM AND TERMINATION

**11.1 Term**

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until all PHI provided by Covered Entity to Business Associate, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, is destroyed or returned to Covered Entity, or if infeasible, protections are extended to such PHI, in accordance with this Section 11.

**11.2 Termination for Cause**

Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within 30 days of receiving written notice of the breach (or such shorter period as may be required by law or regulation).

**11.3 Automatic Termination**

This Agreement shall automatically terminate upon termination of the Services Agreement between the parties, or upon termination of Business Associate's provision of services to Covered Entity.

**11.4 Effect of Termination**

Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Business Associate shall:

a) **Return of PHI**: Return to Covered Entity or, if agreed to by Covered Entity in writing, destroy all PHI received from Covered Entity, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, that Business Associate still maintains in any form. This provision shall apply to PHI in the possession of Subcontractors or agents of Business Associate.

b) **Certification of Destruction**: If Covered Entity agrees to destruction rather than return, Business Associate shall provide Covered Entity with a written certification that the PHI has been destroyed in accordance with the standards set forth in NIST SP 800-88 or other industry-accepted secure destruction methods.

c) **Retention for Legal Purposes**: If return or destruction of PHI is infeasible, Business Associate shall:
   - Notify Covered Entity in writing of the conditions that make return or destruction infeasible;
   - Extend the protections of this Agreement to such PHI and limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make the return or destruction infeasible;
   - Provide Covered Entity with an explanation of the continued use or disclosure and the safeguards that will be maintained.

**11.5 Survival**

The obligations of Business Associate under Sections 4, 7, 8, 10, 11.4, 13, and 14 shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

**11.6 Assistance with Transition**

Upon termination, Business Associate shall reasonably cooperate with Covered Entity to facilitate the orderly transfer of PHI and transition of services to Covered Entity or a successor Business Associate, provided that Covered Entity reimburses Business Associate for reasonable costs incurred in such transition assistance.

### 2. DESTRUCTION OF PHI

**2.1 Timeline**
- Business Associate shall destroy all PHI within **30 days** of termination date
- Extensions may be granted for large datasets upon written request

**2.2 Destruction Standards**
- **Electronic Media:** NIST SP 800-88 Guidelines for Media Sanitization
  - Solid State Drives (SSD): Cryptographic erase or physical destruction
  - Hard Disk Drives (HDD): DoD 5220.22-M (7-pass wipe) or physical destruction
  - Cloud Storage: Cryptographic erasure with key destruction
  - Backups: Deletion of all backup copies or destruction of backup media
- **Paper Records:** Cross-cut shredding to particles ≤ 5mm × 5mm
- **Optical Media:** Physical destruction (crushing, shredding)

**2.3 Scope**
All PHI shall be destroyed, including:
- Production databases and application data
- Voice recordings and audio files
- Backup copies (online and offline)
- Archived data and disaster recovery copies
- Development and testing data containing real PHI
- Logs and audit trails containing PHI
- Any PHI held by Subcontractors

**2.4 Verification**
- Third-party destruction vendor certificates (for physical media)
- Cryptographic erasure verification reports
- Database deletion confirmation with transaction logs
- Backup deletion confirmation from all storage locations

**2.5 Certification of Destruction**
Business Associate shall provide written certification including:
- Statement that all PHI has been destroyed
- Description of destruction methods used for each media type
- Date(s) of destruction
- List of systems, databases, and storage locations from which PHI was destroyed
- Confirmation that Subcontractors have destroyed all PHI
- Copies of third-party destruction certificates (if applicable)
- Name and title of person certifying destruction

### 3. INFEASIBILITY OF RETURN OR DESTRUCTION

If return or destruction of PHI is infeasible:

**3.1 Infeasibility Determination**
Infeasibility may exist when:
- PHI is commingled with other data in backup systems that cannot be selectively deleted
- Legal or regulatory requirements mandate retention (e.g., state record retention laws)
- Technical limitations prevent deletion without destroying non-PHI data
- Cost of extraction exceeds reasonable business expectations

**3.2 Notification**
Business Associate shall provide Covered Entity with written notification including:
- Detailed explanation of why return or destruction is infeasible
- Description of PHI that cannot be returned or destroyed
- Location and format of PHI
- Safeguards that will be maintained
- Duration of continued storage
- Plan for eventual destruction when feasible

**3.3 Continued Protection**
If destruction is infeasible, Business Associate shall:
- Extend all protections of this BAA to the retained PHI
- Limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible
- Implement additional safeguards to protect retained PHI:
  - Logical isolation from operational systems
  - Enhanced access controls (limited to essential personnel only)
  - Enhanced monitoring and alerting
  - Annual review of necessity for continued retention
- Return or destroy the PHI when feasibility is restored
