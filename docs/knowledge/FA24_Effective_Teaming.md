### Welcome to Effective Teaming *(Page)*
Text MM 0 Welcome to Effective Teaming! Module Item Completion (built in browser, hidden in app) Overview Welcome to Effective Teaming. Working on a team – whether co-located with your team members or distributed across the globe – has fast become one of the most common ways for organizing the human capital of firms. It is therefore imperative that the managers of today and tomorrow understand how the many facets of a team, including its processes, structure, and collective mental states, can be mobilized to harness the team’s collective strength. Through real-world examples and hands-on activities, you will learn that different team tasks require different combinations of these elements to achieve optimal performance. Our goal in this course will be to master, in a principled manner, the many aspects of effective teaming so that you can seamlessly and intuitively use them to not only design high-performing teams but also diagnose and intervene in low-performing teams.

### Course Expectations *(Page)*
Text 0 Course Expectations Module Item Completion (built in browser, hidden in app) Expectations This course provides the opportunity to engage in team exercises and simulations, as well as to receive information and feedback about how you work in a group setting, that can inform your understanding of your own strengths and weaknesses as a team member. To fulfill the course expectations for Effective Teaming, participants must actively engage in the coursework and demonstrate a commitment to collaborative learning. First and foremost, students are required to be actively engaged in the course, both in individual assignments as well as in the group project and assignments. Participation is a crucial component of this course as students will learn from each other, share insights, and collectively explore team dynamics. Additionally, it is important to complete all reading assignments and activities on time to contribute meaningfully to the class. Secondly, students will work in teams to complete several group assignments, the most significant of which is the Team Consulting Report (TCR). Each team must select a real or fictional team to analyze and apply course concepts to diagnose problems and recommend solutions. The TCR should be treated as a real-world consulting project, requiring students to gather and analyze data, apply theoretical frameworks, and demonstrate a strong understanding of team dynamics. Collaboration and equitable participation among team members will be evaluated, with the quality of work dependent on the collective effort of each team member. Finally, students must complete individual assignments and the final exam to demonstrate their understanding of the key concepts covered throughout the course. The final exam will consist of multiple-choice and true/false questions based on the readings, cases, and other assignments. To succeed in this course, participants should maintain academic integrity, stay engaged in team activities, and complete all assignments and assessments by the deadlines provided.
