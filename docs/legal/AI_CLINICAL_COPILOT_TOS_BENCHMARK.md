# AI Clinical Copilot — Terms of Service & Privacy Policy Benchmark Analysis

> **Research Date:** March 10, 2026
> **Compiled for:** Cortex / Holi Labs Legal & Compliance Strategy
> **Companies Analyzed:** Ambience Healthcare, Abridge, Nabla, Suki AI, Nuance DAX, Regard, Elation Health, Augmedix

---

## Executive Summary

This document analyzes the legal frameworks of 8 leading AI clinical copilot / health AI companies across 10 key dimensions. The analysis extracts exact legal language and identifies common patterns for structuring Terms of Service, Privacy Policies, and related agreements for AI-powered clinical documentation and decision support tools.

### Key Patterns Observed

1. **Universal "not a medical device / not medical advice" disclaimer** — Every company explicitly distances itself from SaMD classification
2. **Provider retains final clinical responsibility** — Universal pattern placing clinical liability on the licensed healthcare provider
3. **AI output accuracy disclaimed** — All companies disclaim accuracy of AI-generated outputs
4. **12-month fee cap on liability** — Near-universal dollar cap on aggregate liability
5. **Consequential damages excluded** — Universal exclusion of indirect/consequential damages
6. **Business Associate Agreement (BAA) as separate instrument** — All US-focused companies structure HIPAA compliance through a standalone BAA
7. **De-identified / aggregate data rights retained by vendor** — All companies reserve rights to use de-identified data
8. **Mandatory arbitration trending** — Growing adoption of binding arbitration clauses

## 1. LIABILITY LIMITATION LANGUAGE

### How companies frame that the AI/platform is a tool and the provider retains clinical liability

#### Suki AI
> *"CUSTOMER IS RESPONSIBLE FOR FINAL REVIEW OF POST-PROCESSED DATA INCLUDING ANY CLINICAL NOTES, ORDERS, DIAGNOSES AND OTHER CLINICAL DOCUMENTATION AND/OR TRANSCRIPTS GENERATED BY THE SUKI PLATFORM PRIOR TO RELYING THEREON"*

> *"SUKI SHALL NOT HAVE ANY LIABILITY WHATSOEVER FOR THE ACCURACY OR COMPLETENESS OF POST-PROCESSED DATA OR FOR ANY DECISION MADE OR ACTION TAKEN BY CUSTOMER (OR ANY AUTHORIZED USER) IN RELIANCE UPON ANY POST-PROCESSED DATA"*

#### Augmedix (Terms of Agreement)
> *"CUSTOMER HEREBY AGREES AND ACKNOWLEDGES THAT AUGMEDIX IS IN NO WAY ACTING AS A MEDICAL PROVIDER, NOR IS AUGMEDIX PROVIDING 24/7 CONTINUOUS, SYNCHRONOUS, OR EMERGENCY MONITORING OR ALERTING [...] THE ULTIMATE RESPONSIBILITY FOR DIAGNOSING AND TREATING ANY PATIENT RESTS WITH CUSTOMER AND/OR ITS HEALTHCARE PROVIDER(S) TREATING SUCH PATIENT."*

#### Augmedix (General Terms of Use)
> *"THE PLATFORM CANNOT AND IS NOT DESIGNED, INTENDED, OR APPROPRIATE TO REPLACE OR SUBSTANTIVELY IMPACT YOUR PROVIDER-PATIENT RELATIONSHIP [...] OR TO ADDRESS SERIOUS, EMERGENT, OR LIFE-THREATENING MEDICAL CONDITIONS AND SHOULD NOT BE USED IN THOSE CIRCUMSTANCES."*

> *"WE DO NOT PROVIDE MEDICAL ADVICE, AND WE ARE NOT RESPONSIBLE FOR OR IN CONTROL OF THE MEDICAL ADVICE PROVIDED TO YOU BY PROVIDERS, EVEN IF SUCH ADVICE IS PROVIDED THROUGH THE SERVICES."*

#### Elation Health (Service Agreement, §1.6)
> *"Customer acknowledges that the professional duty to the patient in providing healthcare services lies solely with the healthcare professional providing patient care services. As between the Parties, Customer takes full responsibility for the use of all information provided through the Service in providing patient care."*

> *"Elation and its suppliers provide no medical or other professional advice in connection with this Agreement, the Service and the information contained therein. The Parties acknowledge that a licensed professional is responsible for independently reaching any medical or other professional judgment, and for any resulting diagnosis and treatments, notwithstanding any use of the Service by such professional."*

#### Nabla
> *"NABLA assumes no responsibility for any misuse of the Solution."*
> *"NABLA is not responsible for the conditions under which the CLIENT or the Users use the Solution"*
> *"the use of the Solution does not exempt [the CLIENT] from keeping a copy of the Data on any medium of his choice in accordance with his legal and regulatory obligations"*

#### Nuance DAX (via Dictation Direct)
> *"DAX Copilot is a tool to assist healthcare professionals and is not a substitute for professional medical judgment. Users remain responsible for all clinical decisions and documentation accuracy."*

#### Abridge (Clinician Terms)
Platform described as supporting "providers, and provider practices, in patient management through audio records, transcription services" — framed as a support tool, not a clinical decision-maker. Full section headings include "Representations, Warranties, and Disclaimer" and "Limitations of Liability" (content behind collapsed sections at time of research).

## 2. CLINICAL DECISION SUPPORT (CDS) FRAMING — AVOIDING SaMD CLASSIFICATION

### How companies avoid Software as a Medical Device classification

#### Suki AI
Defines itself as: *"SaaS-based artificial intelligence (AI) product that uses machine learning, conversational-AI and process automation to provide medical transcription and other related [services]."*

**Key framing:** Transcription and documentation tool, NOT diagnosis or treatment tool. Avoids words like "diagnose," "detect," "prevent," or "treat."

#### Augmedix (General Terms of Use)
> *"The Services consist of a suite of cloud-based healthcare software solutions [...] supporting clinical, administrative, and operational workflows. These include AI-powered tools for ambient clinical documentation, analytics and clinical decision support, revenue cycle management, patient engagement, and other digital health services. The Services assist healthcare organizations, professionals, and patients in managing care delivery and operations but do not substitute for professional medical judgment."*

**Notable:** Augmedix is the only company that explicitly uses the phrase "clinical decision support" in its ToS. It immediately qualifies this with "do not substitute for professional medical judgment."

#### Elation Health
Defines AI Services as: *"functionality supported by third party artificial intelligence (AI) providers"* — positions AI as a feature layer on top of the EHR, not a standalone clinical tool.

#### Nabla
Defines itself as: *"a digital solution processing health data [...] in the form of a web extension or application that includes artificial intelligence functionalities"*

**Key framing:** A "digital solution" with "AI functionalities" — not a medical device. French legal framework (governed by French law, subject to Paris Commercial Court jurisdiction).

#### Ambience Healthcare
Describes its core business as: *"providing clinicians with technology that enables them to document and transmit information seamlessly into their patients' electronic health records, and perform other healthcare-related functions."*

**Key framing:** Documentation and transmission tool. No clinical decision language.

#### Regard
Positions as "AI Clinical Insights Platform" that "recommend[s] diagnoses" — **this is the most aggressive positioning** among all companies analyzed. Their press materials state they have "recommended over 9 million diagnoses." This phrasing carries higher regulatory risk than competitors.

## 3. AI/ML-SPECIFIC DISCLAIMERS

### How companies disclaim AI-generated outputs

#### Suki AI (strongest language)
> *"CUSTOMER ACKNOWLEDGES THAT THE SUKI TECHNOLOGY IS BASED ON PREDICTIVE STATISTICAL MODELS AND OTHER AI-POWERED TECHNOLOGIES, AND MAY NOT OPERATE IN A COMPLETELY UNINTERRUPTED OR ERROR-FREE FASHION OR WITHOUT LOSS OF DATA"*

> *"THE SUKI PLATFORM MAY INCLUDE FEATURES AND FUNCTIONALITIES THAT ARE POWERED BY AI TECHNOLOGIES AND THAT THE OUTPUT GENERATED BY SUCH TECHNOLOGIES ARE BASED ON ITS TRAINING DATA AND SUKI DOES NOT GUARANTEE THE ACCURACY OR COMPLETENESS OF SUCH OUTPUT"*

#### Elation Health (Service Agreement §8.6 — most detailed)
> *"USE OF THE AI SERVICE MAY, IN SOME SITUATIONS, RESULT IN OUTPUTS THAT DO NOT ACCURATELY REFLECT REAL PEOPLE, PLACES OR FACTS."*

Includes five specific disclaimers:
1. Expressly disclaims all warranties on accuracy, quality, reliability, correctness, completeness
2. Outputs may be incomplete, incorrect, or offensive
3. **Must not use Output for any purpose with legal or material impact on a person** (credit, employment, housing, insurance, legal, **medical** decisions)
4. Customer must evaluate Outputs with human review before using/sharing
5. Outputs should not be sole source of truth or substitute for professional advice

**Notable:** Elation's prohibition against using Outputs for "medical or other important decisions" is the most restrictive among all companies analyzed — and is potentially contradictory to the product's purpose as an EHR with AI features.

#### Augmedix (General Terms of Use — AI section)
> *"AI outputs may be incomplete, inaccurate, or biased. These features are intended to assist, not replace, professional judgment. Users are responsible for verifying the accuracy of any AI-generated content or recommendation before relying upon it. Augmedix disclaims liability for actions taken in reliance on AI-generated outputs. AI-based tools are provided for informational support only."*

#### Elation Health (Terms of Use — user-facing)
> *"ELATION DOES NOT MAKE (AND EXPRESSLY DISCLAIMS) ANY AND ALL WARRANTIES AND/OR REPRESENTATIONS REGARDING THE USE OF THE OUTPUTS, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO, THEIR ACCURACY, QUALITY, RELIABILITY, CORRECTNESS OR COMPLETENESS."*

> *"YOU MUST EVALUATE OUTPUTS FOR ACCURACY AND APPROPRIATENESS FOR YOUR OWN USE CASE, INCLUDING USING HUMAN REVIEW AS APPROPRIATE, BEFORE USING OR SHARING OUTPUTS FROM THE SERVICE."*

## 4. PROFESSIONAL RESPONSIBILITY

### How companies place final responsibility on the licensed healthcare provider

| Company | Key Language |
|---------|-------------|
| **Suki AI** | Customer is "solely responsible for the accuracy and verification of any transcripts and reports generated" |
| **Augmedix** | "THE ULTIMATE RESPONSIBILITY FOR DIAGNOSING AND TREATING ANY PATIENT RESTS WITH CUSTOMER AND/OR ITS HEALTHCARE PROVIDER(S)" |
| **Elation Health** | "the professional duty to the patient in providing healthcare services lies solely with the healthcare professional providing patient care services" |
| **Nuance DAX** | "DAX Copilot is a tool to assist healthcare professionals and is not a substitute for professional medical judgment" |
| **Nabla** | Client is "solely responsible for [...] compliance and conformity of the implementation and use of the Solution under the Regulations and Regulation 2017/745" (EU Medical Devices Regulation) |
| **Abridge** | Clinician Terms frame the platform as supporting "patient management through audio records, transcription services" — positions provider as the actor |
| **Ambience** | "We are not responsible for how your healthcare provider treats your information" — deflects to provider |
| **Regard** | PHI "used solely to provide and maintain the Scribe App's services for healthcare provider clients" — positions as service to provider |

### Common Pattern
Every company uses a two-part structure:
1. **Negative assertion:** "We are not a medical provider / do not provide medical advice"
2. **Affirmative assignment:** "The healthcare professional retains sole responsibility for clinical decisions"

## 5. DATA PROCESSING

### How companies handle patient data flowing through their AI

#### Abridge
> *"Abridge processes Customer Data, which may include Protected Health Information (PHI), solely on behalf of our customers. Our processing of such information [...] is governed exclusively by the terms of our agreements with those customers, including our Business Associate Agreements (BAAs)."*

> *"We are a 'Business Associate' to your provider and process your data solely on their instruction."*

#### Suki AI
> *"Suki will maintain a security program materially in accordance with industry standards that is designed to (i) ensure the security and integrity of Customer data [...] (ii) protect against threats or hazards to the security or integrity of Customer Data; and (iii) prevent unauthorized access to Customer Data."*

Suki explicitly grants itself rights to: *"access Authorized User account(s), including without limitation Customer Data, to respond to service or technical problems and for the purpose of using the Customer Data for system tuning, grammar tuning, training of acoustic models and other models, tools and algorithms."*

#### Augmedix
Customer grants Augmedix *"a limited, non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide license to (i) Use, reproduce, aggregate and modify the Customer Data [...] for Augmedix to provide the Services; (ii) Use or modify the Customer Data to create De-identified Data; and (iii) Use Customer's name, logo, and trademark for marketing purposes upon written consent."*

#### Regard
> *"Audio is transcribed through Amazon Web Services (AWS) Transcribe under a Business Associate Agreement. Transcripts are stored on secure AWS servers and displayed within Regard. Users can optionally merge transcripts with patient notes via OpenAI (also under a Business Associate Agreement)."*

**Notable:** Regard is the most transparent about its specific sub-processors (AWS, OpenAI) and that each has a BAA.

#### Nuance DAX
> *"Protected Health Information (audio recordings and transcriptions) is processed directly by Nuance/Microsoft on HITRUST certified servers, with Dictation Direct having no access to or storage of PHI."*

#### Ambience Healthcare
> *"Our processing of data on behalf of our healthcare provider customers is governed by the agreements we enter into with our customers, which may include Business Associate Agreements as applicable and required under HIPAA."*

#### Nabla
> *"CLIENT remains the data controller or the representative of the data controller and retains full control over the Personal Data it collects and processes, with NABLA acting only as a data processor."*

Hosting is subcontracted to *"a certified health data host within the meaning of Article L 1111-8 of the French Public Health Code."*

## 6. HIPAA BUSINESS ASSOCIATE PROVISIONS

| Company | BAA Structure | Key Details |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| **Abridge** | Standalone BAA at `/terms/business-associate-agreement` | Complies with HIPAA and HITECH Act; processes PHI solely on provider's instruction |
| **Suki AI** | Separate BAA executed between parties (§2.4) | "Suki is a business associate to Customer"; BAA permits PHI transmission |
| **Augmedix** | Business Associate Addendum at `/business-associate-addendum` | Incorporated by reference into Statement of Work; includes cyber liability insurance requirement |
| **Elation Health** | BAA as Exhibit A to Service Agreement (§11.6) | In case of conflict between main agreement and BAA, BAA prevails for PHI matters |
| **Regard** | BAA with both AWS and OpenAI | Each sub-processor has separate BAA; PHI processed under HIPAA standards |
| **Nuance DAX** | PHI processed by Nuance/Microsoft on HITRUST certified servers | Reseller (Dictation Direct) has no access to PHI |
| **Ambience** | BAA as applicable under HIPAA | SOC 2 Type I & II certified; HIPAA compliant |
| **Nabla** | DPA (Data Protection Agreement) or BAA depending on jurisdiction | US clients: BAA; Non-US: GDPR-compliant DPA |

### Pattern
All US-focused companies structure the BAA as a **separate legal instrument** (not embedded in the main ToS) that can be independently updated and governs specifically the handling of PHI. This isolates HIPAA compliance from general commercial terms.

## 7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY — WHO OWNS WHAT

### AI Outputs

| Company | Output Ownership | Model/Algorithm Ownership | De-identified Data |
|---------|-----------------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| **Suki AI** | Customer responsible for Post-Processed Data once submitted to EMR | Suki owns all IP in Suki Technology including improvements based on Feedback | Suki owns all Aggregate Data (de-identified/anonymized) |
| **Augmedix** | Customer owns Customer Data | Augmedix retains all IP in Software and developments | Augmedix owns De-identified Data: *"Augmedix owns all rights, title and interest [...] in such De-identified Data"* |
| **Elation Health** | **Customer owns Outputs** (§6.2): *"As between Customer and Elation, and to the extent permitted by applicable law, Customer owns the Outputs. Elation hereby assigns to Customer all right, title and interest, if any, in and to the Outputs."* | Elation owns all Elation Technology | May use aggregate/anonymized data for improvement |
| **Nabla** | Nabla retains IP in models/algorithms even from data generated by Client use: *"NABLA is and remains the sole owner of all intellectual property rights relating to the models and algorithms of the Solution, including in connection with the use of the Data generated by the CLIENT"* | Nabla owns all IP including models trained on Client data | Free to use Data *"to contribute to the research and development of the Solution"* |
| **Abridge** | Subject to BAA for PHI | Abridge retains platform IP | Subject to Clinician Terms |
| **Regard** | Customer data owned by Customer | Regard retains platform IP | Subject to BAA |

### Key Distinction
**Elation Health is the only company that explicitly assigns Output ownership to the customer.** All others either retain ownership, treat outputs as the customer's responsibility (but not property), or are silent on the issue.

**Nabla has the broadest vendor IP claim** — asserting ownership over models and algorithms even when trained on client data.

## 8. INDEMNIFICATION STRUCTURE

### Customer → Vendor Indemnification (what the customer must indemnify the vendor for)

| Company | Scope of Customer Indemnification |
|---------|----------------------------------|
| **Suki AI** | Excluded Claims: designs provided by Customer, unauthorized use, modifications by non-Suki parties, Customer Data, combination with other applications |
| **Augmedix** | Improper use of Services, breach of agreement/TOU, accuracy of Customer Data, violation of law, **diagnosis and/or treatment of patients**, negligent acts or willful misconduct |
| **Elation Health** | Interactions with Patients/third parties, acts/omissions of Customer, breach of representations, Customer Content |
| **Nabla** | Client responsible for "any consequence related to the conditions of use and operation of the Solution by the Users" |

### Vendor → Customer Indemnification (what the vendor will indemnify the customer for)

| Company | Scope of Vendor Indemnification |
|---------|--------------------------------|
| **Suki AI** | Third-party IP infringement claims only (US patents, copyrights, trade secrets) |
| **Augmedix** | Negligent acts, willful misconduct, IP infringement |
| **Elation Health** | US trade secret, copyright, trademark infringement only |
| **Nabla** | Counterfeiting/IP infringement of pre-existing third-party rights |

### Critical Finding: Augmedix Indemnification
Augmedix is the **only company** that explicitly requires customers to indemnify Augmedix for *"the diagnosis and/or treatment of any of Customer's patients"* — making clinical liability indemnification explicit rather than implied.

## 9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY — DOLLAR CAPS

| Company | Cap | Consequential Damages | Statute of Limitations |
|---------|-----|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| **Suki AI** | Amounts paid during 12 months preceding incident (under applicable SOW) | Excluded (indirect, special, incidental, consequential, punitive) | Not specified |
| **Augmedix (ToA)** | Amounts paid during 12 months preceding claim | Excluded (indirect, incidental, special, consequential, punitive, lost data/profits/revenue) | **2 years** from cause of action |
| **Augmedix (ToU)** | Lesser of **$5,000** or 12 months of fees (in states requiring a cap) | Excluded (incidental, special, exemplary, consequential, **personal/bodily injury, emotional distress**) | **1 year** from occurrence |
| **Elation Health** | Amounts paid during 12 months preceding event | Excluded (special, indirect, incidental, punitive, exemplary, reliance, consequential) | Not specified |
| **Nabla** | Last annual fee collected by NABLA | Excluded (indirect damage, commercial loss, loss of orders, loss of profits, brand image damage) | Subject to French law |
| **Nuance DAX** | Not specified in reseller terms | Excluded (indirect, consequential, punitive) | Not specified |

### Common Pattern
The **12-month trailing fee cap** is the dominant structure. Exceptions to the cap typically include:
- Indemnification obligations
- Breach of confidentiality
- Breach of license restrictions
- Customer payment obligations

### Notable: Augmedix's User-Facing ToU
Augmedix's General Terms of Use (user-facing, not enterprise) includes the most extreme limitation: *"the lesser of $5,000 or twelve (12) months of fees"* — effectively capping liability at $5,000 for most individual users. It also explicitly excludes liability for **personal/bodily injury** and **emotional distress**, which is aggressive for a healthcare platform.

Nabla is the **only company** that explicitly references EU Medical Device Regulation (MDR) 2017/745, placing responsibility on the Client for *"compliance and conformity of the implementation and use of the Solution under the Regulations and Regulation 2017/745 of April 5, 2017 on medical devices."* This is a sophisticated defensive measure — acknowledging MDR exists while making the client responsible for classification compliance.

Augmedix includes a mutual representation that neither party is *"currently excluded, debarred or suspended from participation in any federal health care programs"* — addressing OIG exclusion list compliance, which no other company in this analysis explicitly addresses.

## UNIQUE / NOTABLE CLAUSES

### 1. Suki AI — Training Data Rights
Suki explicitly grants itself the right to use Customer Data for *"system tuning, grammar tuning, training of acoustic models and other models, tools and algorithms"* — the most explicit training data clause among all companies.

### 2. Augmedix — Nonsolicitation
Augmedix includes a **2-year non-solicitation clause** with liquidated damages of **30% of first year's compensation** if the customer hires an Augmedix employee. This is unusual for a SaaS agreement.

### 3. Elation Health — Output Ownership Assignment
Elation is the only company that **affirmatively assigns AI Output ownership to the customer**: *"Elation hereby assigns to Customer all right, title and interest, if any, in and to the Outputs."* With a carve-out that other customers may receive similar Outputs.

### 4. Nabla — French Law Governance
As a French-headquartered company, Nabla's terms are governed by French law with jurisdiction in the Paris Commercial Court — meaning US-based customers must litigate in France.

### 5. Augmedix — Explicit AI Disclaimer Section
Augmedix is the only company with a dedicated, standalone section titled *"Artificial Intelligence and Automated Features"* in its General Terms of Use — suggesting this is becoming a best practice.

### 6. Regard — Sub-Processor Transparency
Regard is uniquely transparent about naming its AI sub-processors (AWS Transcribe, OpenAI) and confirming each has a BAA — a practice that builds trust but increases contractual exposure.

### 7. Elation Health — Contradictory AI Output Restriction
Elation's ToU states: *"YOU MUST NOT USE ANY OUTPUT RELATING TO A PERSON FOR ANY PURPOSE THAT COULD HAVE A LEGAL OR MATERIAL IMPACT ON THAT PERSON SUCH AS [...] MEDICAL [...] DECISIONS ABOUT THEM."* — This is logically contradictory for an EHR platform, as clinical documentation inherently involves medical decisions.

### 8. Suki AI — "Post-Processed Data" Definition
Suki introduces a unique legal concept: *"Post-Processed Data means data created in and/or processed by the Suki Platform and thereafter submitted into Customer's EMR."* This creates a clear liability handoff point — once data enters the EMR, Suki disclaims all liability.

## RECOMMENDED LANGUAGE FOR CORTEX / HOLI LABS

Based on this benchmark analysis, the following clauses should be considered for Cortex's legal framework:

### CDS Positioning (following Augmedix + Suki pattern)
```
The Cortex Platform is a clinical decision support tool that assists healthcare 
professionals with documentation, workflow optimization, and clinical insights. 
The Platform does not substitute for professional medical judgment. The licensed 
healthcare professional retains sole responsibility for all clinical decisions, 
diagnoses, and treatment plans.
```

### AI Output Disclaimer (following Suki + Elation pattern)
```
The Platform utilizes artificial intelligence and machine learning technologies 
that produce outputs based on statistical models and training data. AI-generated 
outputs may be incomplete, inaccurate, or contain errors. Customer is solely 
responsible for reviewing, verifying, and approving all AI-generated content 
before incorporating it into clinical documentation or relying upon it for any 
clinical purpose. Cortex disclaims all liability for any decision made or action 
taken in reliance upon AI-generated outputs.
```

### Liability Cap (following industry standard)
```
In no event shall Cortex's aggregate liability exceed the amounts paid by 
Customer during the twelve (12) month period immediately preceding the event 
giving rise to liability. In no event shall Cortex be liable for any indirect, 
incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, including but not 
limited to loss of profits, data, or goodwill, regardless of the cause of 
action or theory of liability.
```

### Professional Responsibility (following Elation + Augmedix pattern)
```
Customer acknowledges that: (a) Cortex is not a healthcare provider and does not 
provide medical advice; (b) the professional duty to the patient lies solely with 
the licensed healthcare professional; (c) Customer takes full responsibility for 
the use of all information provided through the Platform in providing patient care; 
and (d) a licensed professional is independently responsible for reaching any 
clinical judgment, diagnosis, and treatment plan, notwithstanding any use of the 
Platform.
```
