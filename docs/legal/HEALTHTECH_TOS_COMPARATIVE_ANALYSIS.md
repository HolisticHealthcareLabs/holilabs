# Health Tech / Telehealth — Terms of Service & Privacy Policy Comparative Analysis

**Prepared by:** RUTH (CLO & Regulatory Guardian)  
**Date:** March 10, 2026  
**Purpose:** Inform Cortex / Holi Labs legal positioning for SaaS platform ToS and Privacy Policy drafting

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis covers 7 active health tech / telehealth companies (Babylon Health/eMed was shut down in 2023 and has no current legal pages). For each company, 10 key legal elements are extracted with exact quotes. The universal pattern across all companies is:

1. **Platform-vs-Provider separation** is the single most critical legal architecture
2. **"As Is" + liability caps** are universal ($50-$100 or 12-month fees paid)
3. **Mandatory individual arbitration** with class action waivers is standard
4. **AI disclaimers** are still nascent — most companies do not yet have robust AI/ML-specific language
5. **HIPAA** is referenced but carefully scoped — platforms position as business associates, not covered entities

## 1. RO (ro.co)

**Sources:** ToS (Last revised December 10, 2024), Privacy Policy (Effective September 30, 2025)

### 1.1 Liability Limitation Language

> "IN NO EVENT SHALL RO OR IT'S RELATED PERSONS' TOTAL LIABILITY TO YOU FOR ALL DAMAGES, LOSSES, AND CAUSES OF ACTION (WHETHER IN CONTRACT, TORT (INCLUDING, BUT NOT LIMITED TO, NEGLIGENCE), OR OTHERWISE) EXCEED IN THE AGGREGATE, THE GREATER OF (I) ONE HUNDRED DOLLARS ($100), OR (II) THE AMOUNT PAID BY YOU, IF ANY, FOR ACCESSING THE SERVICES IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTH PERIOD PRECEDING THIS APPLICABLE CLAIM."

Excludes: indirect, incidental, special, consequential, punitive, exemplary damages. Explicitly excludes damages for **personal or bodily injury or emotional distress, including death**.

### 1.2 Disclaimer of Warranties

> "RO AND ITS AFFILIATES, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION ALL AFFILIATED PHYSICIAN PRACTICES, AND THEIR RESPECTIVE OFFICERS, DIRECTORS, MANAGERS, PARTNERS, MEMBERS, EMPLOYEES, AND AGENTS (COLLECTIVELY 'RELATED PERSONS') MAKE NO REPRESENTATIONS OR WARRANTIES AND EXPRESSLY DISCLAIM ANY AND ALL WARRANTIES OF ANY KIND, WHETHER EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, WITH RESPECT TO THE SERVICES INCLUDING, BUT NOT LIMITED TO, ANY REPRESENTATIONS OR WARRANTIES WITH RESPECT TO MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR USE OR PURPOSE, NONINFRINGEMENT, TITLE, AVAILABILITY, SECURITY, OPERABILITY, CONDITION, QUIET ENJOYMENT, VALUE, ACCURACY OF DATA, FREEDOM FROM VIRUSES OR MALWARE, COMPLETENESS, TIMELINESS, FUNCTIONALITY, RELIABILITY, SEQUENCING OR SPEED OF DELIVERY OR SYSTEM INTEGRATION."

### 1.3 Clinical Decision Support / "Not a Healthcare Provider" Framing

> "Ro does not provide any medical services, including via the Services. Rather, Ro provides a technology platform for you to access a health care provider who is employed or contracted with a Ro affiliated physician practice."

> "You understand that by coordinating and consulting with an affiliated physician practice or its health care providers through the Services, you are not entering into a provider-patient relationship with Ro."

> "Except for specific communications received from an affiliated physician practice or its health care providers, none of the information you receive through the Services should be considered medical advice."

**Key architecture:** Ro separates itself from affiliated physician practices. The provider-patient relationship is explicitly between user and the affiliated practice, NOT with Ro.

### 1.4 User Responsibilities

- Must be 18+ (some services 21+)
- Located in a US state where Ro operates
- Responsible for reading all communications and treatment recommendations
- Must maintain accurate account information
- Responsible for all costs (cash-pay only, no insurance billing)
- Must not use modified/jailbroken devices

### 1.5 Data Usage and Sharing

> "You hereby grant Ro... the fully transferable and sublicenseable right and license to use, reproduce, modify, analyze, perform, display, distribute, and otherwise disclose to third parties any data or information you submit on or through the Services for the purposes of providing Services to you; marketing Services to you; conducting research or analyses of such data; and designing, developing, implementing, modifying and/or improving new, current or future features, products and services."

Privacy Policy discloses sharing with: affiliates, healthcare providers, service providers, advertising networks, joint marketing partners, and for legal purposes. Shares health information and sexual orientation data with advertising partners for targeted advertising (can be opted out).

### 1.6 HIPAA/Privacy Compliance

Privacy Policy references CCPA, state privacy laws (California, Colorado, Connecticut, Delaware, Indiana, Iowa, Kentucky, Maryland, Minnesota, Montana, Nebraska, New Hampshire, New Jersey, Oregon, Rhode Island, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Virginia), and Nevada law. **No direct HIPAA reference in the consumer privacy policy** — HIPAA compliance is handled through the affiliated physician practices.

### 1.7 Arbitration/Dispute Resolution

- Mandatory binding arbitration via **NAM (National Arbitration and Mediation)**
- 60-day informal resolution period required first
- Class action waiver
- Jury trial waiver
- **30-day opt-out window** from arbitration
- Batching procedures for mass filings (100+ similar claims)
- Governing law: **Delaware**
- Venue: New York, New York

### 1.8 Indemnification

> "You agree to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Ro, its affiliates, its affiliated health care practices, its subsidiaries, and all of their directors, officers, employees, contractors, licensors, suppliers, representatives, proprietors, partners, shareholders, principals, agents, predecessors, successors, assigns, accountants, and attorneys harmless from and against any and all third-party suits, actions, claims..."

Triggered by: (i) unauthorized use of Services, (ii) fraud, violation of law, negligence, or willful misconduct, (iii) breach of Terms.

### 1.9 AI/ML Disclaimer

> "Ro uses artificial intelligence (AI) technologies to enhance and improve our Services and users' experiences. Specifically, we may use AI technologies for our legitimate business purposes to process the information we collect about you... While AI assists in many processes, critical decisions regarding your health care always involve human oversight from qualified healthcare professionals."

**Notable:** Ro is one of the few companies that explicitly addresses AI in its Privacy Policy, including the statement that "critical decisions regarding your health care always involve human oversight."

### 1.10 Termination Provisions

- User may terminate by discontinuing use
- Ro may terminate "with or without prior notice, for any reason, and at any time"
- Surviving provisions: Disclaimer of Warranties, Limitation of Liability, Indemnification, Governing Law, Dispute Resolution
- After termination, affiliated professional entities retain obligations to provide access to health records

### 1.11 Notable Unique Clauses

- **Cash-pay only model** explicitly prohibiting insurance billing or counting toward deductibles
- **Body Program** weight-loss membership with auto-renewal and specific Zepbound vial provisions
- **Open Payments Database** notice for transparency

## 2. K HEALTH (khealth.com)

**Sources:** ToS (Last modified June 06, 2023), Privacy Policy (fetched)

### 2.1 Liability Limitation Language

> "IN NO EVENT SHALL COMPANY OR ANY AFFILIATED ENTITY BE LIABLE TO YOU FOR ANY CLAIMS, PROCEEDINGS, LIABILITIES, OBLIGATIONS, DAMAGES, LOSSES, OR COSTS IN AN AMOUNT EXCEEDING THE AMOUNT YOU PAID TO COMPANY HEREUNDER IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE DATE ON WHICH THE APPLICABLE CLAIM AROSE, OR $100.00, WHICHEVER IS GREATER."

Explicitly excludes liability for: **"CANCELLED OR OTHERWISE UNFULFILLED APPOINTMENTS OR MEDICAL MALPRACTICE, FRAUD, OR NEGLIGENCE OF PROVIDERS UTILIZED THROUGH THE SERVICE."**

### 2.2 Disclaimer of Warranties

> "THE SERVICE IS PROVIDED ON AN 'AS IS,' 'WHERE AVAILABLE' AND 'AS AVAILABLE' BASIS. USE OF THE SERVICE IS AT YOUR OWN RISK. TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE LAW, COMPANY DISCLAIMS ALL WARRANTIES WITH RESPECT TO THE SERVICE... INCLUDING, BUT NOT LIMITED TO, IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, QUALITY OF INFORMATION, QUIET ENJOYMENT, NON INFRINGEMENT, AND TITLE."

### 2.3 Clinical Decision Support / "Not a Healthcare Provider" Framing

K Health has one of the most explicit and detailed separations:

> "K HEALTH, INC. IS NOT A PROVIDER OF CLINICAL ADVICE."

> "Company and the K Health Subsidiaries do not provide any clinical services nor recommend or endorse any specific Providers, tests, medications, products, or procedures."

> "While K Health Inc. may facilitate your selection of and communication with Providers, K Health, Inc. and the K Health Subsidiaries do not provide clinical services or clinical advice, and any Provider-patient relationship is only between you and the applicable Provider."

> "Neither K Health, Inc. or the K Health Subsidiaries is liable for any professional or other advice you obtain from a Provider via the Virtual Visit Service."

**AI-specific framing:** K Health defines its "Symptom Checker" as an "artificial intelligence-enabled symptom checker" but carefully states:

> "EVEN WHEN ASSESSMENTS ARE BASED ON CLINICAL PROTOCOLS, SUCH ASSESSMENTS DO NOT CONSTITUTE CLINICAL CARE, ADVICE OR DIAGNOSIS."

### 2.4 User Responsibilities

- Must be 18+ (or have parental consent for Additional Users)
- Must provide truthful and accurate information
- Must be physically located in the state entered as their location
- Must upload identification for verification
- Responsible for informing Provider about interactions with other providers
- Must be responsive to ongoing requests for information from care team
- Cannot use Service while driving
- Cannot use for emergency or workers' compensation claims

### 2.5 Data Usage and Sharing

Consent to data collection, use, and disclosure including transfer to US, Israel, or other countries. Licensed K Health to use User Content with "royalty-free, fully paid-up, sublicensable (through multiple tiers), transferable, perpetual, irrevocable, non-exclusive, worldwide license."

### 2.6 HIPAA/Privacy Compliance

References HIPAA through Notice of Privacy Practices. Mentions CommonWell Health Alliance integration with specific PHI handling terms.

### 2.7 Arbitration/Dispute Resolution

- Mandatory individual arbitration via **JAMS**
- 60-day informal resolution first
- Class action/jury trial waiver
- **30-day opt-out window**
- Governing law: **New York**
- Venue: New York County
- **1-year statute of limitations** on claims

### 2.8 Indemnification

Triggered by: (i) use and access to Service, (ii) breach of Terms, (iii) violation of third-party rights, (iv) violation of law, (v) misleading User Content, (vi) negligence or willful misconduct, (vii) third-party access using your credentials.

### 2.9 AI/ML Disclaimer

K Health is one of the most transparent about AI:
- Defines "Symptom Checker" as "artificial intelligence-enabled"
- But explicitly states assessments "DO NOT CONSTITUTE CLINICAL CARE, ADVICE OR DIAGNOSIS"
- No separate AI-specific liability section

### 2.10 Termination Provisions

- Company may terminate "at any time and without prior notice, for any or no reason"
- Extensive list of surviving sections (Sections 1.1-1.6, 1.8-1.12, 2-8, 11-19)
- Must provide access to health records per legal obligations

## 3. GOODRX (goodrx.com)

**Sources:** ToS (Last updated October 16, 2025)

### 3.1 Liability Limitation Language

> "TO THE EXTENT ANY ASPECTS OF THE FOREGOING LIMITATIONS OF LIABILITY ARE NOT ENFORCEABLE, THE MAXIMUM LIABILITY OF GOODRX TO YOU WITH RESPECT TO YOUR USE OF THE SERVICES IS $50 (FIFTY DOLLARS)."

> "GOODRX SHALL NOT BE LIABLE FOR THE ACTS OR OMISSIONS OF THE MEDICAL GROUP OR PROVIDERS."

**Lowest liability cap** of all companies analyzed ($50).

### 3.2 Disclaimer of Warranties

> "ALL CONTENT AND SERVICES ARE PROVIDED 'AS IS' AND 'AS AVAILABLE.' GOODRX DISCLAIMS ALL REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES, EXPRESS, IMPLIED, OR STATUTORY, NOT EXPRESSLY SET OUT IN THESE TERMS, INCLUDING THE IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE AND NON-INFRINGEMENT."

> "GOODRX DOES NOT GUARANTEE THE QUALITY, SUITABILITY, SAFETY OR ABILITY OF THIRD PARTY PROVIDERS, INCLUDING THE MEDICAL GROUP OR PROVIDERS."

### 3.3 Clinical Decision Support / "Not a Healthcare Provider" Framing

> "GoodRx does not provide medical advice or care."

> "The Providers, and not GoodRx, are responsible for the quality and appropriateness of the care they render to you."

> "While GoodRx facilitates your selection of, and communications with, Providers, GoodRx does not provide medical services, and the provider-patient relationship is between you and the Provider."

**GoodRx Care Assistant (AI) specific disclaimer:**

> "The GoodRx Care Assistant is not a healthcare provider and does not provide medical care or a medical diagnosis."

> "ALL OF THE MATERIAL PROVIDED THROUGH THE GOODRX CARE ASSISTANT PORTION OF THE SITE... ARE FOR INFORMATIONAL PURPOSES ONLY AND ARE NOT A SUBSTITUTE FOR PROFESSIONAL MEDICAL ADVICE OR TREATMENT. GOODRX CARE ASSISTANT DOES NOT PROVIDE MEDICAL CARE, ADVICE OR DIAGNOSIS AND IS NOT A QUALIFIED HEALTHCARE PROVIDER."

### 3.4 User Responsibilities

- Must be 18+
- US residents only
- Cannot combine GoodRx with Medicare/Medicaid
- Responsible for all prescription costs at time of purchase
- Must maintain accurate account information

### 3.5 Data Usage and Sharing

"Claims Data" — pharmacy claims data linked to user information for targeted coupons. Grants GoodRx a "worldwide, perpetual, irrevocable, transferable, royalty-free license" to User Content.

### 3.6 HIPAA/Privacy Compliance

GoodRx references "Medical Group Notice of Privacy Practices" for telehealth services. Notable: GoodRx was subject to FTC enforcement action in 2023 for sharing health data with advertisers, leading to significant privacy policy changes.

### 3.7 Arbitration/Dispute Resolution

- **Two-tier system:** Consumer Arbitration (NAM) vs. Business Entity Arbitration (JAMS)
- Mandatory Pre-Arbitration Notice with specific content requirements
- 60-day informal resolution period
- Class action/collective relief waiver
- **Mass Filing procedures** with staged batching (20 claims per side per stage)
- Governing law: **New York**

### 3.8 Indemnification

Triggered by: (i) use of Services, (ii) breach of Terms, (iii) GoodRx's use of User Content, (iv) violation of third-party rights, (v) violation of laws including anti-spamming rules.

### 3.9 AI/ML Disclaimer

GoodRx Care Assistant has explicit non-clinical disclaimer (see 3.3 above). No broader AI/ML policy.

### 3.10 Termination Provisions

- GoodRx may terminate "at any time for any reason"
- **1-year statute of limitations** on all claims
- User may cancel Gold Membership at any time; refund within first 30 days

### 3.11 Notable Unique Clauses

- **Not insurance** — prominent and repeated
- **FTC consent order implications** — heightened privacy obligations post-2023 enforcement
- **Prescription discount framing** — GoodRx does not make payments to pharmacies

> "TELADOC IS NOT RESPONSIBLE OR LIABLE FOR ANY ADVICE, COURSE OF TREATMENT, DIAGNOSIS OR ANY OTHER INFORMATION, SERVICES OR PRODUCTS THAT YOU MAY OBTAIN THROUGH THE WEBSITES."

- Does not engage in the practice of medicine

### 5.3 Emergency Disclaimer

Teladoc is the largest publicly-traded telehealth company and its legal architecture serves as an industry template. The separation between technology platform and clinical services is the most mature in the industry.

## 6. CEREBRAL (cerebral.com)

**Sources:** Privacy Policy (Last revised September 17, 2025), ToS (Retrieved via search — last revised September 17, 2025)

### 6.1 Liability Limitation Language

Services provided on "as-is" basis. Assumes no responsibility for errors or omissions in service contents.

### 6.2 Clinical Decision Support / "Not a Healthcare Provider" Framing

> "Cerebral operates the Platform but does not provide medical services and is not a medical group."

> "Any telehealth consults obtained through our Services are provided by independent medical provider groups, including, but not limited to, Cerebral Medical Group, P.A. (collectively, the 'Medical Groups'), which engage with a network of United States-based health care providers."

> "Cerebral does not own or have any ownership interest in the Medical Groups and the Medical Groups do not own or have any ownership interest in Cerebral."

**Critical legal separation:** Cerebral emphasizes corporate separateness between the technology company and the Medical Groups — no ownership interest in either direction.

### 6.3 HIPAA Compliance

> "While Cerebral is not a 'covered entity' as defined in HIPAA, one or more of the Medical Groups and its Providers, the Pharmacies, the Labs, and/or other health care providers engaging with you through our Services may be a 'covered entity' or 'business associate' under HIPAA and therefore subject to HIPAA, and Cerebral may in some cases be a 'business associate' of a Medical Group."

**Key nuance:** Cerebral carefully qualifies its HIPAA status — "may in some cases be a business associate" — rather than categorically claiming or disclaiming covered entity status.

### 6.4 Data Usage

- De-identification and aggregation rights
- May use biometric information for identity verification
- Collects sensitive data including government IDs, SSN (via verification provider), health information
- Shares with Medical Groups, Pharmacies, Labs, advertising vendors
- CCPA compliance for California residents

### 6.5 Arbitration

- Binding individual arbitration
- Class action waiver
- Emergency services disclaimer prominent

### 6.6 Notable Context

Cerebral faced significant regulatory scrutiny in 2022-2024 including DEA investigations and FTC consent order regarding privacy practices. Post-merger (2025), the privacy policy was updated to reflect new entity names.

## 7. ZOCDOC (zocdoc.com)

**Sources:** ToS (Effective March 29, 2024), retrieved via web search

### 7.1 Clinical Decision Support / "Not a Healthcare Provider" Framing

Zocdoc positions itself as a **technology marketplace** that connects patients with healthcare providers:
- Does not recommend or refer patients to individual providers
- Does not judge or rank providers according to quality
- Marketplace is patient-initiated and patient-directed

### 7.2 Arbitration

### 7.3 Notable Architecture

Zocdoc's model is fundamentally different from the other companies — it is a **scheduling/marketplace platform** rather than a direct telehealth service. This gives it the cleanest liability separation since it never touches the clinical relationship at all.

## Key Patterns & Recommendations for Cortex / Holi Labs

### Pattern 1: The "Platform, Not Provider" Architecture (UNIVERSAL)

Every company uses the same fundamental legal architecture:
1. **Technology company** operates the platform (the entity users contract with)
2. **Affiliated professional corporations / Medical Groups** provide clinical services
3. **Provider-patient relationship** exists only between user and the medical group/provider
4. **No ownership cross-interest** between platform and medical groups (Cerebral is most explicit)

**Recommendation for Cortex:** Maintain strict corporate separateness between the SaaS platform entity and any affiliated clinical organizations. ToS must state: "Cortex does not provide medical services. Cortex provides clinical decision support tools for use by licensed healthcare professionals."

### Pattern 2: Liability Caps are Extremely Low

The industry standard is **$100 or 12-month fees paid** (whichever is greater). GoodRx goes as low as **$50**. All companies exclude:
- Indirect/consequential damages
- Personal injury or emotional distress
- Loss of profits/data
- Medical malpractice by providers

**Recommendation for Cortex:** Use the "$100 or 12-month fees" formula. For B2B enterprise contracts (Track B), negotiate separate liability terms.

### Pattern 3: Arbitration is Standard but Under Pressure

All companies mandate individual binding arbitration with class action waivers. Most use NAM or JAMS. All provide a 30-day opt-out window. Mass filing procedures are becoming standard (Ro and GoodRx both have detailed batching procedures).

**Recommendation for Cortex:** Implement mandatory arbitration with 30-day opt-out. For LATAM jurisdictions (Brazil), note that mandatory arbitration may not be enforceable for consumer contracts under CDC (Código de Defesa do Consumidor).

### Pattern 4: AI Disclaimers are Nascent

Only 3 of 7 companies have explicit AI/ML disclaimers. Ro's is the most mature: "critical decisions regarding your health care always involve human oversight from qualified healthcare professionals." K Health defines its AI Symptom Checker but carefully disclaims clinical value. GoodRx disclaims its Care Assistant.

**Recommendation for Cortex:** This is an opportunity to lead. Draft an AI/ML section that:
1. Defines AI-assisted features as "clinical decision support" (NOT diagnostic tools)
2. States all AI outputs require human professional review
3. Disclaims AI accuracy and reliability
4. Retains clinical liability with the provider
5. References SaMD Class I positioning per RUTH's SaMD Defense protocol

### Pattern 5: Data Usage is Extremely Broad

All companies claim broad rights to user data, including:
- De-identification and aggregation
- Research and analytics
- Advertising (with opt-out)
- Perpetual, irrevocable licenses to User Content

Ro and GoodRx have faced FTC/state AG enforcement for data sharing practices.

**Recommendation for Cortex:** For LATAM markets, LGPD and LPDP require **granular consent** (per RUTH's mandate). Do NOT replicate the broad US-style blanket licenses. Implement consent toggles for: Service, Research, Marketing, and Analytics.

### Pattern 6: Emergency Disclaimers are Universal

Every company prominently disclaims emergency use:
> "IF YOU THINK YOU MAY HAVE A MEDICAL EMERGENCY, CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY."

**Recommendation for Cortex:** Implement at ToS level AND in-app UI (per PAUL's UX standards).

## SaMD Defense Implications

**RUTH ASSESSMENT:** None of the analyzed companies use the words "diagnose," "detect," "prevent," or "treat" in their ToS or marketing to describe their technology platforms. All carefully use:
- "informational purposes only"
- "clinical decision support"
- "does not constitute medical advice"
- "for use by healthcare professionals"

K Health comes closest to SaMD risk with its "AI-enabled symptom checker" but carefully disclaims diagnostic value.

**Cortex Position:** Maintain strict SaMD Class I (Clinical Decision Support) positioning. Never describe the platform as providing diagnoses, detecting conditions, preventing disease, or treating patients. The platform SUPPORTS healthcare providers in making their own clinical decisions.
