# RISK-013 Mitigation Runbook
## Pilot Reputation Protection & Reputational Risk Management

**Risk ID:** RISK-013
**Category:** Reputational
**Severity:** Medium
**Owner:** VICTOR (relationship management), ELENA (prediction monitoring), RUTH (contract language)
**Effective Date:** March 2026
**Review Cadence:** Weekly (during pilot phase), monthly thereafter

---

## Executive Summary

During the 90-day pilot validation phase, a materially wrong simulation prediction (e.g., TLP predicts 30 P1 arrivals and ED receives 90) could generate negative clinical word-of-mouth that propagates through Brazil's tight ~200-person medical director network before we have validation data to contextualize the error. This runbook provides the operational framework to manage pilot expectations, monitor prediction accuracy, and execute rapid response protocols if a major prediction miss occurs.

**Key Principle:** Pilot predictions are *research-phase outputs for validation purposes only* and must never influence operational clinical decisions during the validation period.

The Cortex Swarm Layer (CSL) is a population health AI simulation engine. It uses
historical patient data, current hospital census, and clinical knowledge to forecast

- Predict individual patient triage categories or outcomes
- Replace clinical judgment for individual patient care
- Guarantee prediction accuracy during calibration (weeks 1–8)

1. **Weeks 1–4:** Calibration phase. CSL ingests your hospital's triage history.

## 5. Crisis Communication Plan — Major Prediction Miss Response

### 5.1 Escalation Triggers

A "major miss" requiring immediate crisis response is defined as:

- **Prediction error >40%** for a critical triage level (P1 or P2) in a single 4-hour window
- **OR:** A miss that the Medical Director reports as clinically concerning (e.g., "we staffed for 20 P1s and got 60, this caused operational chaos")
- **OR:** A miss that occurs within the first 14 days of pilot (heightened sensitivity due to calibration risk)

### 5.2 24-Hour Crisis Response Protocol

**Hour 0 (Discovery — within 2 hours of the miss becoming apparent)**

1. **Triage Classification:** ELENA classifies the miss as one of:
   - **Tier 1 (Critical):** P1 error >50%, operational disruption reported, risk of negative word-of-mouth
   - **Tier 2 (High):** P1/P2 error 40–50%, operational disruption but manageable
   - **Tier 3 (Medium):** P3/P4 error >40%, minimal operational disruption

2. **Immediate Notification:** ELENA notifies:
   - VICTOR (account lead for hospital)
   - RUTH (legal/reputational counsel)
   - If Tier 1: also notify CEO/founder

3. **Hospital Outreach:** VICTOR calls Medical Director within 1 hour of discovery to:
   - Acknowledge the prediction miss
   - Apologize for the error
   - Ask: "What was the actual operational impact?"
   - Commit to a root cause analysis by EOD

**Hour 1–6 (Root Cause Analysis)**

4. **ELENA's Investigation:**
   - Pull simulation logs, seed data, and actual triage records
   - Identify whether the error was due to:
     - Data quality issue (bad seed data)
     - System error (model failure, hallucination)
     - External event not captured (accident, weather, etc.)
     - Natural high-variance scenario (legitimate uncertainty)
   - Document findings in a preliminary root cause report

5. **Hospital Collaboration:**
   - ELENA contacts Hospital medical director's data steward
   - Share preliminary findings: "The error appears to be due to [ROOT CAUSE]. Here's what we're doing about it."

**Hour 6–24 (Communication & Remedy)**

6. **ELENA's Remediation Plan:**
   - Propose a fix (e.g., "We'll adjust the model's P1 variance; we expect error to reduce by 10% next week")
   - Propose a validation plan (e.g., "Let's run a calibration drill this week with your last 30 days of data")

7. **VICTOR's Stakeholder Management:**
   - If Tier 1: prepare a brief "incident report" for the hospital's Chief Medical Officer
   - Frame the narrative: "This was an edge case during calibration. We've identified the issue and adjusted the model. Expected error bands are back on track."
   - Offer a goodwill gesture if appropriate (e.g., extend pilot period by 1 week free, no impact on final conversion terms)

8. **RUTH's Legal & Reputation Protection:**
   - Confirm that the pilot agreement's liability release (Section 1.2) applies
   - Prepare a statement for the Medical Director: "The pilot agreement explicitly anticipated wide error bands during weeks 1–4. This miss is within the expected error range for a calibration phase."
   - If hospital staff have already shared the miss with other hospitals: Issue a brief clarification memo (cc'd to hospital legal) explaining the research phase context

9. **Internal Documentation:**
   - Log the incident in AuditLog with full root cause analysis
   - Update ELENA's weekly post-mortem (Section 4.2) with the incident details
   - Flag in risk register if miss indicates a systematic model issue (not just an isolated edge case)

### 6.1 Recovery Phases

- Script: "You may have heard about a prediction miss at [Hospital A]. This was during their calibration week. Here's the full context..." (provide pilot agreement excerpt, error band expectations, root cause analysis)
- Goal: Frame the miss as *expected research-phase noise*, not a system failure

**Phase 2: Validation Acceleration (Weeks 5–8)**

- ELENA expedites validation timeline: Run daily post-mortems rather than weekly
- Goal: Show rapid error reduction (from 38% week 1 → 18% week 5) to demonstrate the system is learning
- Publish "Calibration Curve" chart showing error bands tightening over time

**Phase 3: Clinical Storytelling (Weeks 9–12)**

- ELENA prepares validation case study: "Hospital A: 90-Day Calibration to Operational Prediction"
- Highlight: instances where CSL correctly predicted unusual demand (e.g., "system flagged a high P2 load on a Tuesday despite normal staffing patterns, which turned out to accurately predict a surge in pediatric respiratory cases")
- Story repositions the early miss as a "calibration growing pain" rather than a system failure

**Phase 4: Anchor Customer Conversion (Day 90+)**

- Hospital A becomes a success story: "After 90-day validation, prediction error is <12%. Hospital A is integrating TLP into ED staffing decisions and reports 15% improvement in staffing efficiency."

- Goal: Flip narrative from "the AI made a big error early on" to "the AI was research-phase, now it's production-grade"
