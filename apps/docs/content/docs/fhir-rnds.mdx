Native integration with Brazil's national health data network and international standards.

## Standards

**FHIR R4** — Full HL7 FHIR R4 compliance for resource exchange, search, and operations.

**RNDS** — Brazil's Rede Nacional de Dados em Saúde. Patient summaries, immunization records, lab results.

## Clinical Ontologies

| Standard | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| ICD-10 | Diagnosis coding |
| SNOMED CT | Clinical terminology |
| LOINC | Lab and clinical observations |
| RxNorm | Drug interaction checking |
| DCB | Brazilian drug code (ANVISA) |
| TUSS | Brazilian billing codes |

Dual mapping between international ontologies and Brazilian-specific codes enables both clinical accuracy and local billing compliance.

## Portable Health Identity

Cortex's FHIR R4 architecture enables a portable, longitudinal, cryptographically verified health identity that follows the person, not the institution. When a patient moves between cities or countries, their cardiologist's notes, their latest eGFR, their allergy list, and their current medications move with them—not as a PDF export, but as a live, structured, interoperable data stream.

**The health identity belongs to the person, not the provider.**
