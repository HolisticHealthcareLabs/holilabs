Tamper-evident. Hash-chained. Immutable.

Built on Bemi (PostgreSQL WAL-level auditing). Each entry contains the cryptographic hash of the previous entry. Any alteration is immediately detectable.

## Entry Schema

```json
{
  "entryId": "audit-2026-04-06-001",
  "previousHash": "sha256:a1b2c3...",
  "action": "OVERRIDE_ALERT",
  "actor": "dr.silva@clinic.br",
  "justification": "Patient allergy resolved...",
  "timestamp": "2026-04-06T14:23:00Z",
  "hash": "sha256:d4e5f6..."
}
```

## Retention

Audit logs are retained indefinitely per LGPD Article 37. They **cannot** be deleted by erasure requests. When a patient exercises their right to erasure, only the patient-controlled encryption key is destroyed—the audit record of every access to that patient's data remains.

## LGPD `accessReason` Field

Every audit entry records an `accessReason` field documenting the legal basis for data access. This satisfies LGPD Art. 37 accountability requirements and enables regulatory audit responses.

## Override Audit

When a clinician overrides a safety alert, the override is recorded with:
- The alert that was overridden
- The structured justification provided
- The clinician's identity and role
- The timestamp and session context
- The hash linking it to the chain

Override patterns are surfaced in the Governance Console for systematic review.
