Brazil's General Data Protection Law. Built in, not bolted on.

## Key Articles

### Art. 7 — Legal Bases

Every processing operation records its legal basis. Consent is granular (Service/Research/Marketing). Legitimate interest documented with balancing tests.

### Art. 9 — Sensitive Data

Health data classified as sensitive. Requires explicit consent or legal basis. All records encrypted at rest with versioned keys.

### Art. 18 — Data Subject Rights

Access, correction, deletion, portability, consent withdrawal. Right to Be Forgotten with audited erasure workflows. Habeas Data endpoints:

- `GET /api/patients/[id]/export` — full data export
- `POST /api/patients/[id]/deletion-request` — erasure request
- `POST /api/patients/[id]/erasure` — cryptographic key destruction

### Art. 33 — Cross-Border Transfers

Patient data stays in deployment region by default. Cross-border transfers require explicit consent and documented adequacy assessment.

### Art. 37 — Record Retention

Audit logs retained indefinitely. Cannot be deleted by erasure requests. Hash-chain integrity prevents tampering. This is a CYRUS-enforced invariant—no code path can remove audit records.

## Cryptographic Erasure

When a patient exercises their right to erasure, Cortex destroys the patient-controlled encryption key. The data becomes cryptographically inaccessible without physical deletion. Audit logs of the erasure itself are preserved per Art. 37.
