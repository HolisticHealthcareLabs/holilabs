## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Covered Entity is a "Covered Entity" as defined by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, as amended ("HIPAA"), and is subject to the requirements of HIPAA;

WHEREAS, Business Associate provides healthcare technology services to Covered Entity that may involve the use or disclosure of Protected Health Information (PHI);

WHEREAS, the parties desire to enter into this Agreement to ensure compliance with HIPAA, the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act ("HITECH Act"), and their implementing regulations, including the Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules (collectively, the "HIPAA Rules");

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements herein contained, the parties agree as follows:

## 1. DEFINITIONS

Terms used but not otherwise defined in this Agreement shall have the meanings given to them in 45 C.F.R. Parts 160 and 164 (the "HIPAA Rules").

### 1.1 Business Associate
"Business Associate" means HoliLabs Inc., a person or entity that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Covered Entity for functions or activities regulated by HIPAA.

### 1.2 Covered Entity
"Covered Entity" means a health plan, healthcare clearinghouse, or healthcare provider who transmits health information electronically in connection with transactions for which HHS has adopted standards.

### 1.3 Protected Health Information (PHI)
"Protected Health Information" or "PHI" means information that:
- Relates to the past, present, or future physical or mental health or condition of an individual
- Relates to the provision of healthcare to an individual
- Relates to the past, present, or future payment for healthcare provided to an individual
- Identifies the individual or there is a reasonable basis to believe the information can be used to identify the individual

### 1.4 Electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI)
"Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI" means PHI that is transmitted by or maintained in electronic media.

### 1.5 Individual
"Individual" means the person who is the subject of PHI and includes a person who qualifies as a personal representative in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.502(g).

### 1.6 Required by Law
"Required by Law" means a mandate contained in law that compels an entity to make a use or disclosure of PHI and that is enforceable in a court of law.

### 1.7 Secretary
"Secretary" means the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services or their designee.

### 1.8 Security Incident
"Security Incident" means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system.

### 1.9 Breach
"Breach" means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under the HIPAA Privacy Rule which compromises the security or privacy of the PHI.

### 1.10 Unsecured PHI
"Unsecured PHI" means PHI that is not rendered unusable, unreadable, or indecipherable to unauthorized persons through the use of a technology or methodology specified by the Secretary.

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

### 2.1 Permitted Uses and Disclosures
Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only:
- As necessary to perform the services set forth in the underlying service agreement
- As required by law
- As otherwise permitted by this Agreement
- For Business Associate's proper management and administration, provided the disclosure is required by law or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient that the PHI will be held confidentially

### 2.2 Prohibited Uses and Disclosures
Business Associate shall not:
- Use or disclose PHI in any manner that would violate the HIPAA Rules if done by Covered Entity
- Use or disclose PHI for purposes other than those permitted by this Agreement or the underlying service agreement
- Sell PHI without Covered Entity's and Individual's written authorization
- Use PHI for marketing purposes without Covered Entity's and Individual's authorization
- Disclose PHI to a health plan for payment or healthcare operations purposes if the Individual has paid out-of-pocket in full and has requested the restriction

### 2.3 Safeguards
Business Associate shall:
- Implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of PHI other than as permitted by this Agreement
- Comply with the applicable requirements of the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart C) with respect to ePHI
- Implement reasonable and appropriate policies and procedures to comply with the standards and implementation specifications of the HIPAA Security Rule
- Ensure that any agent or subcontractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Business Associate agrees to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to Business Associate
- Report to Covered Entity any use or disclosure of PHI not permitted by this Agreement
- Report to Covered Entity any Security Incident of which it becomes aware

### 2.4 Minimum Necessary
Business Associate shall:
- Make reasonable efforts to use, disclose, or request only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose
- Limit PHI access to workforce members who need access to perform their duties

### 2.5 Mitigation
Business Associate shall mitigate, to the extent practicable, any harmful effect that is known to Business Associate of a use or disclosure of PHI by Business Associate in violation of this Agreement.

## 3. BREACH NOTIFICATION

### 3.1 Discovery and Reporting
Business Associate shall:
- Report to Covered Entity any Breach of Unsecured PHI without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 15 calendar days after discovery of the Breach
- Provide Covered Entity with sufficient information to meet Covered Entity's breach notification obligations under 45 C.F.R. § 164.410

### 3.2 Information to be Provided
Business Associate's breach notification shall include, to the extent possible:
- Identification of each Individual whose Unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed by Business Associate to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed
- A brief description of what happened, including the date of the Breach and the date of discovery
- A description of the types of Unsecured PHI involved in the Breach
- The steps Individuals should take to protect themselves from potential harm
- A brief description of what Business Associate is doing to investigate, mitigate, and prevent future occurrences
- Contact information for Individuals to ask questions or learn additional information

### 3.3 Cooperation
Business Associate shall cooperate with Covered Entity to enable Covered Entity to comply with its breach notification obligations.

### 3.4 Breach Investigation
Business Associate shall:
- Conduct a thorough investigation of the Breach
- Document the investigation and findings
- Provide investigation results to Covered Entity upon request

## 4. SUBCONTRACTORS AND AGENTS

### 4.1 Business Associate Agreements
Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractor or agent to whom Business Associate provides PHI:
- Agrees in writing to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to Business Associate under this Agreement
- Implements reasonable and appropriate safeguards to protect PHI
- Reports any Security Incidents or Breaches to Business Associate

### 4.2 Responsibility
Business Associate is responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors and agents to the same extent as if they were acts or omissions of Business Associate.

### 4.3 List of Subcontractors
Business Associate shall maintain a current list of subcontractors with access to PHI and provide such list to Covered Entity upon request.

## 5. ACCESS TO PHI

### 5.1 Individual Access Rights
Business Associate shall:
- Provide access to PHI in a Designated Record Set to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to an Individual within 15 days of receiving a request from Covered Entity
- Provide access in the form and format requested by the Individual if readily producible, or in an alternative form and format agreed upon by Business Associate and the Individual
- Provide access in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.524

### 5.2 Amendment Rights
Business Associate shall:
- Make any amendments to PHI in a Designated Record Set as directed by Covered Entity within 30 days of receiving a request
- Ensure that amendments are made in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.526

### 5.3 Accounting of Disclosures
Business Associate shall:
- Document all disclosures of PHI and information related to such disclosures as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.528
- Provide to Covered Entity or an Individual an accounting of disclosures within 30 days of receiving a request
- Provide information for the six years prior to the date of the request or from the date this Agreement became effective, whichever is sooner
- Exclude from the accounting disclosures made for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations

### 5.4 Designated Record Set
Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to Covered Entity or the Secretary for purposes of determining Covered Entity's compliance with HIPAA.

## 6. OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

### 6.1 Authorizations and Consents
Covered Entity shall:
- Obtain all necessary authorizations and consents from Individuals for Business Associate to use or disclose PHI
- Notify Business Associate of any restrictions on use or disclosure agreed to by Covered Entity
- Not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would violate HIPAA Rules

### 6.2 Notice of Privacy Practices
Covered Entity shall provide Business Associate with a copy of its Notice of Privacy Practices and notify Business Associate of any changes.

### 6.3 Permission to Use and Disclose
Covered Entity warrants that it has obtained all necessary permissions and authorizations to permit Business Associate to use and disclose PHI as contemplated by this Agreement.

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 7.1 Term
This Agreement shall become effective on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until terminated in accordance with this Section or until the underlying service agreement is terminated, whichever is sooner.

### 7.2 Termination for Cause
Covered Entity may terminate this Agreement and the underlying service agreement immediately if:
- Business Associate breaches a material term of this Agreement
- Covered Entity determines that Business Associate has violated a material term of this Agreement and cure is not possible

### 7.3 Opportunity to Cure
Before terminating for cause, Covered Entity shall:
- Provide written notice of the breach to Business Associate
- Provide Business Associate with 30 days to cure the breach (if cure is possible)
- If cure is not possible or Business Associate fails to cure within 30 days, Covered Entity may terminate immediately

### 7.4 Automatic Termination
This Agreement shall automatically terminate upon termination of the underlying service agreement.

### 7.5 Effect of Termination
Upon termination of this Agreement:
- Business Associate shall return or destroy all PHI received from or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity
- Business Associate shall not retain any copies of PHI
- If return or destruction is not feasible, Business Associate shall extend the protections of this Agreement to the PHI and limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible

### 7.6 Survival
The obligations of Business Associate under this Section 7.5 shall survive termination of this Agreement.

## 8. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF PHI

### 8.1 Upon Termination
Within 30 days of termination of this Agreement, Business Associate shall:
- Return all PHI to Covered Entity in the format specified by Covered Entity, OR
- Destroy all PHI and certify in writing to Covered Entity that the PHI has been destroyed

### 8.2 Destruction Standards
Destruction shall be accomplished by:
- Overwriting electronic media at least three times
- Degaussing or physically destroying electronic media
- Shredding paper records
- Using other methods that render PHI unusable, unreadable, and indecipherable

### 8.3 Retention for Legal Purposes
If Business Associate is required by law to retain PHI, Business Associate shall:
- Notify Covered Entity of the legal requirement
- Extend the protections of this Agreement to the retained PHI
- Return or destroy the PHI when the legal requirement ends
- Limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible

### 8.4 Subcontractor PHI
Business Associate shall ensure that any PHI held by subcontractors or agents is returned or destroyed in accordance with this Section.

## 9. INDEMNIFICATION

### 9.1 By Business Associate
Business Associate shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Covered Entity from and against any and all claims, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from:
- Business Associate's breach of this Agreement
- Business Associate's violation of HIPAA Rules
- Wrongful use or disclosure of PHI by Business Associate or its subcontractors
- Security Incidents or Breaches caused by Business Associate's failure to implement appropriate safeguards

### 9.2 By Covered Entity
Covered Entity shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Business Associate from and against any and all claims, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses arising from:
- Covered Entity's breach of this Agreement
- Covered Entity's failure to obtain necessary authorizations or consents
- Instructions from Covered Entity that cause Business Associate to violate HIPAA Rules

### 9.3 Procedure
The indemnified party shall:
- Promptly notify the indemnifying party of any claim
- Cooperate fully in the defense of the claim
- Allow the indemnifying party to control the defense and settlement

## APPENDIX A: DESCRIPTION OF SERVICES

The Business Associate shall provide the following services to Covered Entity:

1. **Electronic Health Records (EHR) Platform:** Cloud-based EHR system for storing and managing patient health information
2. **Telemedicine Services:** Secure video conferencing for remote patient consultations
3. **Clinical Decision Support:** AI-powered tools to assist with diagnosis and treatment planning
4. **Patient Portal:** Secure portal for patients to access their health information
5. **Appointment Scheduling:** System for scheduling and managing patient appointments
6. **Prescription Management:** Electronic prescribing and prescription tracking
7. **Secure Messaging:** HIPAA-compliant messaging between providers and patients
8. **Data Analytics:** Aggregated and de-identified data analytics for quality improvement

## APPENDIX B: TECHNICAL AND ORGANIZATIONAL MEASURES

Business Associate implements the following safeguards:

### Technical Measures
- Encryption: AES-256 for data at rest, TLS 1.3 for data in transit
- Access Controls: Role-based access control (RBAC) with multi-factor authentication
- Audit Logging: Comprehensive logging of all access to PHI
- Automatic Logoff: Session timeout after 15 minutes of inactivity
- Data Backup: Daily encrypted backups with 30-day retention
- Disaster Recovery: Recovery Time Objective (RTO) of 4 hours

### Organizational Measures
- HIPAA Training: Annual training for all workforce members
- Background Checks: Pre-employment screening for all staff
- Incident Response Plan: Documented procedures for security incidents
- Business Continuity Plan: Procedures for maintaining operations during disruptions
- Vendor Management: Due diligence and BAAs with all subcontractors
