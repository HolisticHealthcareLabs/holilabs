## WHAT IS TELEMEDICINE?

Telemedicine is the use of electronic communications and information technologies to provide or support clinical care at a distance. This includes:

- **Live Video Consultations:** Real-time video appointments with healthcare providers
- **Store-and-Forward:** Transmission of medical images, test results, or reports for review
- **Remote Patient Monitoring:** Electronic transmission of health data (e.g., blood pressure, glucose levels)
- **Mobile Health (mHealth):** Use of mobile devices for healthcare communication and monitoring
- **Secure Messaging:** HIPAA-compliant electronic communication with your healthcare team

## TELEMEDICINE SERVICES WE OFFER

We provide the following telemedicine services:

- **Virtual Consultations:** Video appointments for medical evaluations, follow-ups, and chronic disease management
- **E-Visits:** Secure messaging for minor health concerns
- **Prescription Management:** Electronic prescribing and medication refills
- **Remote Monitoring:** Review of home health data transmitted electronically
- **Second Opinions:** Remote consultation with specialists
- **Mental Health Services:** Telepsychiatry and teletherapy sessions

## BENEFITS OF TELEMEDICINE

- **Convenience:** Receive care from home, work, or while traveling
- **Reduced Travel:** No need to travel to a medical facility
- **Time Savings:** Less time away from work or family
- **Increased Access:** Access to specialists who may not be locally available
- **Continuity of Care:** Easier follow-up appointments and monitoring
- **Reduced Exposure:** Minimize exposure to infectious diseases in waiting rooms
- **Cost Savings:** May reduce transportation and childcare costs

## LIMITATIONS OF TELEMEDICINE

You should understand the following limitations:

**Clinical Limitations:**
- Physical examinations are not possible via telemedicine
- Some diagnoses may be more difficult without hands-on examination
- Certain tests and procedures cannot be performed remotely
- Emergency situations require in-person care (call 911)
- Some conditions are not appropriate for telemedicine evaluation

**Technical Limitations:**
- Technology failures may interrupt or prevent consultations
- Poor internet connection may affect video or audio quality
- Security breaches are possible despite our safeguards
- Equipment malfunction may occur

**Licensing Limitations:**
- Providers must be licensed in the state where you are located at the time of service
- International telemedicine may not be available
- Some insurance plans may not cover telemedicine services

## APPROPRIATE USES OF TELEMEDICINE

Telemedicine is appropriate for:

- Routine follow-up appointments
- Chronic disease management (diabetes, hypertension, etc.)
- Minor acute illnesses (colds, flu, minor infections)
- Medication management and refills
- Mental health counseling and therapy
- Post-operative follow-up
- Preventive care counseling
- Review of test results
- Second opinions

## WHEN TELEMEDICINE IS NOT APPROPRIATE

Seek in-person care for:

- **Emergencies:** Chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, loss of consciousness
- **Severe Symptoms:** High fever with altered mental status, severe pain, sudden weakness
- **Injuries:** Suspected fractures, severe lacerations requiring stitches
- **Initial Evaluations:** First visit for complex new symptoms may require in-person evaluation
- **Procedures:** Any procedure requiring hands-on intervention

**CALL 911 OR GO TO THE NEAREST EMERGENCY ROOM FOR MEDICAL EMERGENCIES**

## PROVIDER RESPONSIBILITIES

Your healthcare provider will:

- Determine if your condition is appropriate for telemedicine
- Conduct a thorough medical evaluation within the constraints of telemedicine
- Recommend in-person evaluation if necessary
- Maintain the same standard of care as in-person visits
- Document the telemedicine encounter in your medical record
- Follow up as clinically appropriate
- Maintain privacy and confidentiality
- Use secure, encrypted technology for all communications

## YOUR RESPONSIBILITIES

As a patient, you are responsible for:

**Before the Appointment:**
- Having necessary equipment and reliable internet connection
- Finding a private, quiet location for the appointment
- Testing your technology before the appointment
- Having a list of current medications and known allergies
- Being in a state where your provider is licensed

**During the Appointment:**
- Verifying your location at the start of the visit
- Providing accurate and complete health information
- Following the provider's instructions
- Asking questions if you don't understand
- Ensuring privacy (no unauthorized persons present without provider consent)

**After the Appointment:**
- Following the treatment plan as prescribed
- Scheduling follow-up appointments as recommended
- Seeking in-person care if symptoms worsen or don't improve
- Reporting any technical issues or concerns

## PRIVACY AND SECURITY

**Our Safeguards:**
- HIPAA-compliant video conferencing technology
- End-to-end encryption of video and audio
- Secure data transmission and storage
- No recording without explicit consent
- Access controls and authentication
- Regular security audits and updates

**Your Privacy Responsibilities:**
- Use a private location for video visits
- Don't share your login credentials
- Log out after each session
- Use secure internet connections (avoid public Wi-Fi)
- Don't allow unauthorized persons to participate in visits

**Recording:**
- Sessions may be recorded ONLY with your explicit consent
- You will be notified if recording is requested
- You may request recording for your records (subject to provider approval)
- Recordings are part of your medical record
