## YOUR RIGHTS IN DETAIL

### 1. Right to Get a Copy of Your Health and Claims Records

**You can ask to see or get a copy of your health and claims records and other health information we have about you.**

What you can request:
- Your medical records
- Billing records
- Claims information
- Visit notes
- Test results
- Images and reports

How to request:
- Submit a written request to: privacy@holilabs.com or mail to [address]
- We will provide a copy or a summary of your health and claims records, usually within 30 days of your request
- We may charge a reasonable, cost-based fee for copies

We may deny your request in certain limited circumstances:
- If the information is compiled for legal proceedings
- If it would endanger you or another person
- If it references another person (unless that person is a healthcare provider)

If we deny your request, you have the right to request a review of that decision.

### 2. Right to Correct Your Health and Claims Records

**You can ask us to correct your health and claims records if you think they are incorrect or incomplete.**

How to request an amendment:
- Submit a written request to: privacy@holilabs.com
- Include the reason for your request
- We will respond within 60 days

We may deny your request if:
- The record was not created by us
- The information is not part of the records we keep
- You would not be permitted to inspect or copy the record
- The record is accurate and complete

If we deny your request, you have the right to submit a statement of disagreement that we will add to your record.

### 3. Right to Request Confidential Communication

**You can ask us to contact you in a specific way or to send mail to a different address.**

Examples:
- Call you at work instead of home
- Send mail to a P.O. Box instead of your home address
- Communicate via email instead of phone

How to request:
- Submit your request to: privacy@holilabs.com
- Specify how or where you wish to be contacted
- We will accommodate all reasonable requests
- We will not ask you the reason for your request

### 4. Right to Ask Us to Limit What We Use or Share

**You can ask us not to use or share certain health information for treatment, payment, or our operations.**

Examples:
- Don't share information about a specific treatment with family members
- Don't share information with your health plan for services you paid for in full out-of-pocket

Your rights:
- We are not required to agree to your request
- If we do agree, we will comply with your request unless the information is needed for emergency treatment
- If you paid for a service or item out-of-pocket in full, you can ask us not to share that information with your health plan, and we must agree

How to request:
- Submit a written request to: privacy@holilabs.com
- Specify what information you want to limit
- Specify who you want to limit sharing with

### 5. Right to a List of Those With Whom We've Shared Your Information

**You can ask for a list (accounting) of the times we've shared your health information for six years prior to the date you ask.**

The accounting will include:
- To whom we disclosed your information
- The date of the disclosure
- A brief description of the information disclosed
- The purpose of the disclosure

The accounting will NOT include disclosures:
- Made to you or your personal representative
- Made with your authorization
- For treatment, payment, or healthcare operations
- To correctional institutions or law enforcement
- That are part of a limited data set
- Made for national security purposes
- Made before April 14, 2003

How to request:
- Submit a written request to: privacy@holilabs.com
- Specify the time period (up to 6 years)
- The first accounting in a 12-month period is free
- We may charge a reasonable fee for additional accountings
- We will respond within 60 days

### 6. Right to Get a Copy of This Privacy Notice

**You can ask for a paper copy of this notice at any time, even if you have agreed to receive the notice electronically.**

How to get a copy:
- Download from our website: www.holilabs.com/legal/hipaa-notice
- Request via email: privacy@holilabs.com
- Request by mail: [address]
- We will provide a copy promptly

### 7. Right to Choose Someone to Act for You

**If you have given someone medical power of attorney or if someone is your legal guardian, that person can exercise your rights and make choices about your health information.**

What we require:
- A copy of the medical power of attorney or court order
- Verification that the document is valid and current
- The personal representative must prove their identity

How to designate:
- Provide documentation to: privacy@holilabs.com or [mailing address]
- We will verify the documentation before granting access

### 8. Right to File a Complaint

**You can complain if you feel your privacy rights have been violated.**

How to file a complaint:

**With Us:**
- Contact our Privacy Officer: privacy@holilabs.com
- Phone: [Privacy Phone]
- Mail: [Address]

**With the Federal Government:**
- U.S. Department of Health and Human Services
- Office for Civil Rights
- Website: www.hhs.gov/ocr/privacy/hipaa/complaints/
- Phone: 1-877-696-6775
- Mail: See website for regional addresses

## YOUR CHOICES

For certain health information, you can tell us your choices about what we share. If you have a clear preference for how we share your information in the situations described below, talk to us. Tell us what you want us to do, and we will follow your instructions.

### In These Cases, You Have Both the Right and Choice to Tell Us to:

**1. Share information with your family, close friends, or others involved in your care**

You can:
- Give us permission to share
- Ask us not to share

If you are not able to tell us your preference (for example, if you are unconscious), we may share your information if we believe it is in your best interest. We may also share information to help with disaster relief.

How to express your choices:
- Tell your healthcare provider
- Complete a form designating who can receive information
- Submit written instructions to: privacy@holilabs.com

**2. Include your information in a hospital directory**

If you are admitted to a hospital, you can:
- Agree to be listed in the directory
- Ask not to be listed in the directory

The directory may include:
- Your name
- Your location in the facility
- Your general condition (e.g., fair, stable)
- Your religious affiliation

Who can access the directory:
- People who ask for you by name
- Members of the clergy (who will receive your religious affiliation even if they don't ask for you by name)

**3. Provide mental health care**

If you receive mental health services, you can:
- Give permission to share information with others involved in your care
- Ask us not to share information

We will share information only with your authorization, except in emergencies or as required by law.

### In These Cases, We Never Share Your Information Unless You Give Us Written Permission:

**1. Marketing purposes**

We will not use or share your information for marketing purposes without your written authorization.

Marketing includes:
- Communications about products or services
- Promotional materials
- Communications from third parties

Exception: We may communicate about our own services without authorization (e.g., appointment reminders, treatment alternatives).

**2. Sale of your information**

We will never sell your information without your written authorization.

**3. Most sharing of psychotherapy notes**

We will not share psychotherapy notes without your written authorization, except:
- As required by law
- For our own training programs
- To defend against a lawsuit by you

**4. Substance abuse treatment records**

Information about substance abuse treatment is subject to additional federal protections under 42 C.F.R. Part 2 and will not be shared without your written authorization, except in very limited circumstances.

### In These Cases, We May Share Your Information:

**1. Fundraising activities**

We may contact you for fundraising efforts. You have the right to opt out of receiving fundraising communications.

How to opt out:
- Click "unsubscribe" in fundraising emails
- Contact: fundraising@holilabs.com
- Phone: [Phone]

## OUR USES AND DISCLOSURES

### How We Typically Use or Share Your Health Information

**1. To Treat You**

We can use and share your health information to provide, coordinate, or manage your healthcare and related services.

Examples:
- A doctor treating you for a broken leg asks another doctor about your overall health condition
- We share your information with pharmacies to fill your prescriptions
- We share your information with laboratories to order tests
- We coordinate care with specialists
- We send appointment reminders

**2. To Run Our Organization**

We can use and share your health information to run our practice, improve your care, and contact you when necessary.

Examples:
- We use information to improve the quality of care
- We contact you to remind you about appointments
- We train staff on providing better service
- We review the care provided to you
- We conduct quality improvement activities
- We perform business planning and development

**3. To Bill for Your Services**

We can use and share your health information to bill and get payment from health plans or other entities.

Examples:
- We bill your health insurance for services rendered
- We provide information to your health plan to determine coverage
- We collect payment from you or your insurance
- We work with collection agencies for unpaid bills
- We verify your insurance coverage and benefits

### How Else Can We Use or Share Your Health Information?

We are allowed or required to share your information in other ways – usually in ways that contribute to the public good, such as public health and research. We have to meet many conditions in the law before we can share your information for these purposes.

**1. Help with Public Health and Safety Issues**

We can share health information about you for certain situations such as:
- Preventing disease and controlling disease outbreaks
- Reporting disease, injury, or vital statistics (births, deaths)
- Reporting adverse reactions to medications or problems with medical products
- Notifying people of recalls of products they may be using
- Reporting suspected abuse, neglect, or domestic violence
- Preventing or reducing a serious threat to anyone's health or safety

**2. Do Research**

We can use or share your information for health research when:
- An Institutional Review Board (IRB) has reviewed the research proposal
- We have appropriate safeguards in place
- We use only de-identified information, OR
- You provide written authorization

**3. Comply with the Law**

We will share information about you if state or federal laws require it, including with:
- The Department of Health and Human Services to investigate our compliance with federal privacy law
- State health departments as required by state law

**4. Respond to Organ and Tissue Donation Requests**

We can share health information about you with:
- Organ procurement organizations
- Eye or tissue banks
- Transplant coordinators

Purpose: To facilitate organ, eye, or tissue donation and transplantation

**5. Work with a Medical Examiner or Funeral Director**

We can share health information with:
- A coroner or medical examiner to identify a deceased person or determine the cause of death
- Funeral directors to carry out their duties

**6. Address Workers' Compensation, Law Enforcement, and Other Government Requests**

We can use or share health information about you:

**Workers' Compensation:**
- For workers' compensation claims
- To comply with workers' compensation laws
- For similar programs providing benefits for work-related injuries

**Law Enforcement:**
- In response to a court order, subpoena, warrant, summons, or similar process
- To identify or locate a suspect, fugitive, material witness, or missing person
- About the victim of a crime if the victim agrees or we cannot get the victim's agreement
- About a death we believe may be the result of criminal conduct
- About criminal conduct at our facility
- In emergency circumstances to report a crime

**Special Government Functions:**
- For military and veterans' activities
- For national security and intelligence activities
- For protective services for the President or others
- For correctional institutions regarding inmates
- For government programs providing public benefits

**7. Respond to Lawsuits and Legal Actions**

We can share health information about you:
- In response to a court or administrative order
- In response to a subpoena, discovery request, or other lawful process (if efforts have been made to notify you or to obtain a protective order)

**8. For Public Health Activities**

We can share your information for public health activities:
- To report births and deaths
- To report child abuse or neglect
- To report adverse events or product defects
- For disease prevention and control
- For public health surveillance
- For public health investigations

**9. To Avert a Serious Threat to Health or Safety**

We may use and disclose your information when necessary to prevent a serious and imminent threat to:
- Your health or safety
- The health or safety of the public or another person
- Law enforcement or others able to prevent or lessen the threat

**10. For Law Enforcement Purposes**

We may disclose your information to law enforcement:
- As required by law (e.g., reporting gunshot wounds)
- In compliance with a court order, warrant, subpoena, or summons
- To identify or locate a suspect, fugitive, material witness, or missing person
- About a crime victim (in limited circumstances)
- About a death that may have resulted from criminal conduct
- About criminal conduct at our facility
- In an emergency to report a crime

- Maintain the privacy and security of your protected health information

- Not use or share your information other than as described here unless you tell us we can in writing
- If you tell us we can, you may change your mind at any time by notifying us in writing
